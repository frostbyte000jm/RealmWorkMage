Rowena Collaborative Adventure

Stories

Heroes:: Rowena (Black and light blue), Barikhol (Blue), Tyelknowle (green), Storyteller (red)

Rowena kicked her feet lazily back and forth as they dangled inches above the gravelly road. The back of the wagon she sat in bumped along, jostling her lightly as the caravan sauntered slowly towards its next checkpoint. She casually brushed a displaced lock of her silvering hair behind her softly pointed ear, then pulled her pack into her lap to rummage around for something. Humming quietly to herself, she seemed to have found what she was looking for, but a sudden jolt caused her to drop it back in the pack; she barely caught herself from tumbling into the road. The wagon had stopped abruptly, the wheel having fallen into an inconspicuous rut which seemed to have cracked the axle. Shouts and curses arose from the front of the caravan as people wandered over to take a look at the damage.

 

The clatter of horse's hooves and the shouts of men echoed almost as loudly as the thundering in Barikhol 's head. The last batch of ale had been potent enough that he felt as if an army of dwarves was working a forge inside his skull.

 

As he opened his eyes, he found leaves closing in around him. "Odd" he thought, why had he woken up in a bush? The last thing he remembered was drinking his latest brew while walking towards town to resupply. Potent indeed.

 

The sound of shouting and cursing grew louder, and finally Barikhol could stand it no more. What were these people doing, running a caravan by his bush?! He crawled out from under the low hanging shrubs, and there was indeed a caravan. It looked like one of the wagons had broken down, and was leaning at an odd angle.

 

He approached the caravan to see what the problem was, and approached a young elf maid. She told Barikhol that the wagon had landed in a rut and something had snapped. Being a dwarf, and having the mechanical aptitude and craftiness of his race, Barikhol climbed under the wagon to take a look.

 

The axle had broken in half, but only a part of the diameter was splintered. The other part had clean saw marks, which had served to weaken the axle. It was enough to get the wagon far from town on the rough roads before it splintered. Why would someone sabotage a wagon so it broke down in the wild? This was surely no day for a hangover.

 

Joclyn had made excellent time chasing after the caravan and Tyelknowle knew he had to hurry if he was going to help stop Joclyn from making a terrible decision . Nicoli had been more than irate, a member of the Mud Blood , Joclyn , working with common highwaymen was bad enough, but sabotaging wagons on the road was horrible business that would come back to haunt everyone. Too much attention, too many blatant robberies, too much death and we would be lucky if just local authorities showed up. More likely adventurers looking for a bounty or just loot and everyone on the road would be suspect and targets. Nicoli rammed this point into Tyelknowle and he knew she was right.

 

Tyelknowle spotted the ambush from behind moving up the game trail. Joclyn would be down low ready to confuse the worst of the fighters. As a tiefling, she had a knack for magic and loved showing up more “muscle types” as she put it.

 

The wagon had been stopped for only a couple of minutes and some dwarf had bent down to crawl underneath. The attack would happen soon, at the worst possible time. The young elf was less stealthy than normal. He ran the risk of being confused for one of the bandits, but he had to get… “NOW! Your goods or your lives!” a bandit cried out. It was too late Ty thought .

 

Tyelknowle saw at least 20 men jump from both sides of the road. A gang that big had to hit a lot of targets or else not enough got paid and that meant internal fighting. It also meant they weren’t usually very well trained or else they would have simply relied on mercenary work, much more profitable and stable. He happened to be only a few feet from one of the archers so he slipped out his rapier and slipped it up the left side, just above the hip crossing to the upper right, puncturing at least one lung and all sorts of other organs that he was sure were needed. There was more yelling, muffled, the dwarf under the wagon hadn’t been surprised and maybe that was enough for the others to at least get a weapon out or run. Running was always better in this situation .

 

Ty couldn’t see clearly, so he moved closer to another waiting archer. This archer he stabbed just under the right lower ribs and up. Closer than he liked, but just as effective. The bandits were confident and thus not aware that two of their number had been dropped before the fight had truly started. There was always a few seconds where most bandits watched to see if their prey would fight or flee. Tyelknowle would not be good in a straight up fight, and his goal was the tiefling. He stabbed down the spine of a third bandit who looked ready to charge and found himself at the edge of the road.

 

The targets had not ran, but they seemed a bit confused. There were at least two other people making a ruckus on the other side and nobody seemed sure what was happening. At the same time he spotted Joclyn so did the dwarf (still under the wagon). Tyelknowle , seeing some of the caravans horses, was not about to rob anyone but he had to get her out of here and quickly. Also, how much longer could he remain unseen by both sides? As long as he could stop his adopted sister, maybe they had someone he that could help stop all this, maybe; Damn his luck...

 

Joclyn was preparing to cast one of her fog spells, which would have been the advantage the bandits needed to wipe out the caravan. Barikhol , seeing all of this encounter from under his wagon and yelled out “young elf maid, help him!” while nodding toward the rogue elf quietly taking out highwaymen and trying to make his way to the tiefling. Barikhol threw his wrench at Joclyn breaking her concentration on the fog.

 

Startled by a garbled cry of, “Now! You’re good for a prize!” Rowena stepped out from behind the wagon to see several highwaymen initiates charging at the caravan. Leading the charge was a bizarre looking… woman?

 

Oh, a tiefling. How interesting. Rowena had time to muse, before throwing forward her arms to send a spell at the marauders and halt their attack.

 

Sleep. Sleep sleep sleep sleep…. She bit her lip in concentration. Hadn’t quite gotten the hang of this one, yet…

 

Suddenly, a swarm of flumphs appeared around her. Oh. Not quite what I was going for… The floating creatures scattered, fleeing in terror.

 

Seeing a swarm of floating jellyfish creatures heading straight for them, the startled highwaymen broke off their charge in confusion. “Someone has summoned demons from another plane!” When the lead flumph reached the bandits, it shot out a foul smelling spray, dousing the devilish looking woman in a disgusting smelling liquid. Several of her comrades began hacking and gagging. As they backed away from their fallen, fetid friend, the rest of the flumphs continued to rush past the bandits. Rowena ran up to the downed bandit, who seemed to have been completely disoriented.

 

Sleep! Rowena ’s thoughts raged, and the tiefling fell over, unconscious. Rowena ’s gaze lifted from the woman laying at her feet to stare wistfully at the retreating pack of flumphs. Oh well. Maybe I’ll catch one next time…

 

As Rowena bent down check the tiefling’s condition, she slowly realized that she was not alone. “You didn’t kill her. Thank you for that. She’s just… misguided,” said a man’s voice next to her. Rowena looked up to see another highwaymen, his hand casually resting on the hilt of his sword.

 

“Well,” Rowena said nervously, eyeing the man, “I try to make that more of a last resort…”

 

“The name is Tyelknowle .” He began rummaging through his pack, searching for something.

               

“ Rowena .” She responded, trying to peer at what he was looking for. “What are you doing…?”

 

Tyelknowle produced a rope from his pack and began tying up the tiefling. “Looking for this.”

 

“Oh. I’m working on the incarcerous spell. My grandmother told me a story and their wizards used it. It would have been pretty helpful right now.” Footsteps behind her caused Rowena to turn and miss the elf’s confused look.

 

“Ereything a’right here, lass?” the bush-dwarf growled.

 

“Yep! Tyelknowle is tying up his buddy here. Who are you, by the by?” Rowena smiled.

 

“ Barikhol . Charmed.” He said, eyeing over the elf.

 

“Well, then. Shall we get back to fixing that wheel?”

 

“You’re just gonna let this devilwoman walk?”

 

“Well, not exactly. She’ll be asleep for a bit longer, so I don’t think she’ll be walking anywhere…”

 

Tyelknowle unsheathed his sword and stepped in front of the tiefling. “Please. Let me take her. My guild will ensure she meets just punishment.”

 

“Now now,” Rowena hedged, “No need to swing that pointy thing around. And I don’t mean your ears. But you had better get going before the caravan leader finds you.

 

As Tyelknowle gets up to take his sister out of here he whispers "Thank you" and turns around with his sisters arm over his shoulder starts heading towards the woods.

 

Suddenly, a hand grabs his arm firmly and he spins around ready for whatever comes at him. Tyelknowle was a little surprised to see Rowena stopping him. "Your sister is the cause of many people getting injured. If any of them died today she would not be leaving here. Promise me she will be properly dealt with."

 

Tyelknowle could see Rowena was vacillating with her decision on letting them go. "I will, promise." he told her.

 

Rowena must have decided to believe him because she let them go.
