Downtimes FB 06

 

Armond and Barikhol was sitting in a tavern. After many drinks Armond had that newbie confidence that most apprentices get. Barikhol was quizzing Armond on the brewing process. Armond was doing well and with every correct answer he would gain a little more confidence.

 

One thing lead to another and all of a sudden both Armond and Barikhol was at a brewers place of business. At some point in the night a bewer… berooer… Drink-maker joined them and Barikhol honestly couldn't tell you how they got here.

 

Armond was trying to put together a batch. He was making a mess. It was hilarious. Barikhol and their Drink-Maker friend was whispering loudly to each other watching Armonds every action.

 

The next morning. Barikhol and Armond came by to apologize to the brewer and help him clean up. They worked together making another batch and give real instruction, vs drunken criticism to Armond . The brewers name was Poss and he had some interesting techniques. Barikhol and him shared some of their experiences and they all had a good laugh when Barikhol told the story of the Fire Mead. It has been long enough to where this story is now funny.

 

Poss offered up some advice for next time and Barikhol would consider it, but probably wouldn't attempt it again till he is sure it will work.

 

A week later they got to try the mead they made. The first one was Armonds Drunken Drink. The moment the drink hit Barikhol 's and Poss's lips they were spitting it out. It was one of the worst drinks Barikhol had ever tasted, and that is saying something. Armond on the other hand was still drinking it.

 

"Not bad", Armond said. Both Barikhol and Poss was looking at each other confused. "Maybe it is an acquired taste."

Downtimes 05

 

"We are the bringers of Woe , we have come to reward your curiosity." a blob of mud comes shooting up from the ground and wraps itself around Barikhol 's face. Barikhol scratches at it and grabs a clump by the fist. Piece after piece come off the mud, but it doesn't seem to let go. The mud must be over his eyes, because it is dark and he can't see anything.

 

Barikhol takes a swing. "WACK!" solid hit, and he is pretty sure he broke something. He tries again, "CRACK". Barikhol is pretty sure that was a rib. If Barikhol goes down, it won't be without breaking something. The only problem is, everyone needs air.

 

Barikhol was high up on the mountain. He has been sparring with Master Oboku . The fight had been going on for a while now. They had to balance themselves on top of these stones. If one got knocked off the stone they lost. Barikhol finally slipped in his footing and was tipped over by Master Oboku .

 

"How did I beat you?" Master Oboku inquired.

 

"I am exhausted. I can't breathe in this elevation." Barikhol replied.

 

"Ahh, yes." Master Oboku responded, "Air. Everyone needs air. Why am I not defeated by air then?"

 

"You control the air." Barikhol replied. Barikhol knew that Master Oboku was an air genasi, but Master Oboku never talked about it so he never thought to bring it up.

 

"You think my heritage gives me an advantage? Perhaps." Master Oboku confirmed. "Breathing is an essential part to martial arts. If you focus enough, you can control it. Let me show you."

 

Master Oboku worked with Barikhol on breathing. This training took a long time many tendays, but Barikhol noticed that by the end of the training he could control his breathing well.

 

That is when Master Oboku attacked him with a ball of air. The ball wrapped itself around Barikhol 's head and started pulling the air away from his face. Barikhol was suffocating. He swung for the magical sphere, but there was nothing to grab. Oboku was yelling at him. "Do not try and control the air, control what breath you have left!"

 

Barikhol stopped and placed his hands together like he does when he is meditating. He was able to hold his breath for what seemed like an eternity.

 

Barikhol realized something at the moment when the mud was wrapped around his head. These guys have had plenty of time to kill him. If they were going to do it, they would have by now. Knowing his options were low, Barikhol threw up his hands in a defensive position. When he realized they were not attacking, he placed them together like he would if he was meditating.

 

He felt them drag him off, but eventually succumbed to the lack of oxygen and passed out.

 

Barikhol woke up in a tomb. They were tied to a stone and they could hear someone walking around. Looking around there were at least 4 other bodies that looked like they were wrapped up for burial down here.

 

"You're awake. One of my men saw you questioning Mother Yalantha" This mystery man said to Barikhol , "and we felt you were getting to close. So we brought you two down here where it will be safe… Well it will be safe for us. Tell us what you know about the elemental eye."

 

Barikhol didn't know much about the eye, and probably wouldn't have told them anything if he did. From what he gathers these men were afraid that the eye sent Barikhol and his party here to remove them. Barikhol remembers some of their questions from the moments they started torturing him.

 

"Let me tell you a story." begins Master Oboku . Barikhol and Master Oboku were eating lunch still up on top of the mountainside. They were taking a break from monk lessons for now. "A long time ago, there was this man, Langtee. He would travel from place to place to help out the poor, the sick, and the helpless. He didn't have much, but he was able to do things to make life a little more bearable for these people. Some people were able, with the little help from Langtee, to pull themselves out of the terrible place they were in. They would go to serve Langtee, but Langtee would refuse their service. He would instead say, 'there are others like you that need help. Find them, help them, and take nothing for it. When you have done this Ilmater will bless you. ' They would go and do as he bid. Langtee was visiting a town ran by an evil king. The king would over tax and over punish his people. The kingdom feared him and his army. Langtee knew of this king and the king knew of him."

 

"The King invited Langtee to the castle and informed him that he has been helping those being punished by the king. Langtee said 'I help those who are suffering and whose hearts are good.' The king said to Langtee that he must leave his kingdom and never return. Langtee said to the king, 'I cannot. There are still many who suffer here and I must do what I can.'

 

The king grew angry with Langtee and had his guards take him away.

 

Barikhol wakes up with a severe pain on his chest. The Earth Cult are now crushing him under a bolder that should flatten him by all accounts. The leader of the group, Larrakh , was holding the rock with some kind of elemental magic. "Tell me what you know about Marlos Urnrayle! Why are you here? How do you know about us? What do you know about the awakening?"

 

Down in the dungeons Langtee was thrown into a shared cell. Langtee was going to person to person, summoning water and healing the injured. He saw these people suffered greatly under the king and he was going to do what he could to help.

 

The king got word that Langtee was helping others in the dungeon and ordered his men to bring him back. Moments later Langtee was standing in front of the king again. The king said 'Your crime is aiding those that are being punished by order of the king. Your punishment is to be whipped 10 lashes.'

 

Langtee was strung up and started reciting: "Persevere in the face of pain. Heal the sick, the wounded, and the diseased. Comfort the dying, the grief-stricken, and the heartsick. Take on the burdens and the pain of others. Champion the causes of the oppressed and unjustly treated, and give shelter and kind counsel to the lonely, the lost, and the ruined. Pursue the service of Ilmater, and he will provide—leave gross riches and the acquisition of all but medicines to others. Take up the tasks no others dare."

 

He was struck 10 times. Langtee cried out in pain, but he spilled very little blood. After his whipping. He was taken back down to the dungeons. Langtee walked on his own. The king was amazed. He had never seen someone walk away from a whipping.

 

Barikhol could feel the dull cuts against his skin. He was popping in and out of consciousness. "Tell us where you buried the necromancer." Larrakh asked.

 

"The Necromancer?" Barikhol thought. He didn't even remember telling them about the necromancer.

 

"Where was his source of power?" Larrakh demanded. As he stabbed harder into Barikhol 's side.

 

Barikhol replied in a whisper "Persevere in the face of pain. Heal the sick, the wounded, and the diseased. Comfort the dying, the grief-stricken, and the heartsick…"

 

Langtee returned to the prisoners and continued helping and healing in any way he could. The prisoners begged him to take care of himself first, but Langtee refused.

 

The king was furious. He demanded that his guard bring Langtee to the flames. The flames was a large fireplace that was closed off with a magical window. The king and the kingdom could watch the criminal be burned alive. Langtee was placed inside this box. Langtee said to the king, "I am not the only one. You will never defeat the work that has been done in the name of Ilmater."

 

The King said through his teeth. "The people you help are my people to punish. Your god has no control here. You will now die for your crimes."

 

Langtee looked around. He was a little nervous, but he was ok with his death. His eyes landed on another man in the box with him. "King! Burn me if you will, but let this other man go. No man deserves this fate."

 

The King was confused, he asked his guards "is there someone else in the box?" the guard answered there was not. The king now believing Langtee was crazy, this explains why Langtee did those things against the kings orders. "If anyone in the box, they shall suffer the same fate as you."

 

Langtee grew scared, but not for himself, for the man that was in the box with him. "I am sorry." Langtee said to the man.

 

"it is alright. Do not fear for me, for I am here for you. Your suffering has been enough." The man said to Langtee. Langtee seeing the man for the first time realized this man has been through more suffering than anyone could bare. The man placed a hand on Barikhol 's shoulder "You have done enough. I will take over from here."

 

The flames ignited and Langtee stood there. He felt nothing. His cloths burned off, but not a lick of his skin was touched. The king outraged that Langtee is living inside this box commanded the guards make the box hotter. They did, but it wasn't enough. They made the box so hot it became unstable and exploded.

 

They searched, but never found Langtee's body. Many years later the king was laying on his sick bed when he was approached by one of his servants. "You have made people suffer for your entire life. Now it is your turn to receive every inch of suffering you have given." The king looked at his servant, discussed that he even speak to him, but went wide eyed when he noticed the servant was Langtee. "I was sent here to pass a gift from Ilmater on to you."

 

Langtee whispered something in the kings ear and touched him on his forehead. The king arched his back from pain, and endured all the torcher he ever placed on another person back to back. His body survived the torcher, but died once completed. This gift lasted years for the king, but his punishment is the punishment of Ilmater.

 

Barikhol woke up hearing the words "You have suffered enough." whisper near his ear.

 

"What?" Barikhol couldn't quite make out what was said.

 

Larrakh sees Barikhol chained up to the rock. Turns to him and says "Your friends came for you. Looks like you have been saved, this time." and Larrakh escapes through a panel in the wall.

 

A little over an hour later Barikhol wakes up unharmed. He can see cuts and bruises from where he was tortured, but he was uninjured. Barikhol is back at his room at Swinging Sword .

Barikhol gave his name in response, "Right, Barikhol . Have a good one."

Downtimes FB 02

 

Armond and Barikhol still had a long travel ahead of them to meet with Josephus and Wyer . They took their time with their travels, since there was nothing time pressing and one of the reasons to become an adventurer is to see the world.

 

Eventually the two came across a farm. It has been a long day’s journey and they may not have sun for much longer. The heroes were starting to notice that more and more frightening creatures were coming out at night the further they traveled. Not that the two didn't run into troubles along the way with goblins, kobolds, and a few random undead creatures. It was just noticeably more dangerous to be out at night the closer they got to waterdeep and the north.

 

When the two approached the farm they noticed the farmer, a half elf by the name of Lorfeanor. Lorfeanor is pleasant in appearance, with cropped white hair and soft gray eyes. He wears travel-stained clothing and several pouches hang from his belt. Lorfeanor was out cleaning up from a day's work. Barikhol offered the half-elf to share a bottle of wine that he made.  You three got to talking and Lorfeanor mentioned that his barn is actually a brewery. He said "My father is a legend in making beer. Almost every pub in the sword cost keeps at least a keg stocked of 'Pure Hoppiness'. I still make it and distribute it." he then asks, "Do you happen to know much about the machinery used to make beer? You see something seems to be wrong with by brew kettle and I was never good at fixing these things. My father built this along with an expert metal worker. He would have known exactly what to do."

 

After a little bit of evaluation Barikhol tells the Lorfeanor that Armond and himself can fix this. Barikhol explains that Armond is his apprentice and this is actually good training to learn how this process works. This equipment requires a lot of maintaining and it appears that the half elf has not been keeping up on this regularly. The solution, or at least where they should start, is that they need to breakdown and clean out all the equipment. From there they should notice exactly what is wrong.

 

Lorfeanor says "How about I give you a room and fine cooked meals every morning and night. I will even help you with this and pay for any supplies." Barikhol and Armond , knowing this is good training, agree to this and they get to work the next morning.

 

Barikhol would stop and explain things to Armond and Lorfeanor. It was kind of like a class and Barikhol was the instructor. Barikhol would comment how this setup was a work of art. Barikhol has seen many breweries in his travels, but never a machine as finely fashioned as this. This is probably why it didn't require as much maintenance and the machine held out this long before it desperately needed service.

 

About three days into working on the brewery, they realized they will need supplies. The Half Elf invited Barikhol and Armond to travel to town with him to make sure he is getting the right materials. About five miles out of town the three travelers saw a struggle. It appeared like someone was getting robbed and that the robber was using some kind of magic. Barikhol and Armond looked at each other and silently exchanged acceptance that they should do something.

 

The heroes jumped off their transport and made their way by placing the victim wagon between the robber and them. This way they have a better chance at getting close before being seen. Lorfeanor stayed back and out of the way. He is no fighter and would probably get in the way, more than aid the two heroes. Once the heroes got closer they could hear they robber. "A toll is due from any man that crosses this path. Failure to pay the told is an act of treason against the mud army. You sir tried to run, you tried to trick, you tried to steal from my lord Maraj Vizann (see Downtime 01). Now all your equipment and your life are forfeit. Do you have any last words?"

 

"Maybe, but you will never hear them." Barikhol said while looking over at Armond for approval of a good entrance liner. Armond quickly fist bumps Barikhol (Alright you two can change this piece if you want to, I am just imagining two 'not quite level 01s' and how they are still finding themselves). Armond takes aim at the sorcerer and lets his arrow fly true while Barikhol goes running and sliding for the mud slingers legs.

 

The mud slinger manages to form a shield with the dirt around him to barley block the arrow, but not quick enough to feel a pop in his knee and a sharp paid down his leg. The Mud slinger spins around to face Barikhol and forms arrows from the earth. This reminds Barikhol of his training with Master Oboku where many small stones would be launched at the dwarf and he had to anticipate where they would land so he could move just enough out of the way. This was not Barikhols favorite practice, but it came in handy now and he was able to roll, twist, grunt loudly, and turn just barely out of the way. A few cuts and scrapes is a much better outcome than what it could have been.

 

Another Arrow flies from Armonds bow and strikes through the mud slingers stomach. He pukes dirt and arches his back from the impact. Sand blew off towards the north east into the sky as the last breath left his lungs.
