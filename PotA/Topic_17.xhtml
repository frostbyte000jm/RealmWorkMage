Game 09

Game Day

Game Date: 06/21/16

Date In Play:: (5) Mirtul 06, 1491; (5) Mirtul 07, 1491;

TLDR

  • The Air Genasi wouldn’t tell the Mortals anything important.
  • Once Ty enchanted him he tried to talk, but he really didn’t know anything.
  • The only thing they learned was the Air Cult was trying to infiltrate the Feathergale Spire .
  • The prisoner killed himself with his chains.
  • The Heroes traveled south towards Feathergale Spire . On the way they stopped at the Vale of Dancing Water. It is a Dwarven Shrine. They gave them a place to stay the night.
  • The next morning the heroes continued their journey.

Detailed Entry

The heroes woke with the sun. Most of them made their way into the prisoners room. On entry they realized he is something different. The prisoner was blue skinned.

 

This reminded Ty of his days with the mud bloods . There were two twin sisters that had that same skin color. They had a minor connection to the wind. Kind of like this guy.

 

They started asking him a few questions. The prisoner served a master and wouldn't speak with the "mortals" except to toy with them.

 

Finally Ty enchanted the creature. He became friendly and wanted to try and be helpful. The problem was he really didn't know much. His master, who he couldn't give up his name even if he tried, didn't let him in on a master plan.

 

What the heroes did get was they was the air cult was trying to infiltrate Feathergale Spire .

 

After they went to talk with the survivor. He didn't know much other than they were attacked by a tornado, giant vultures, and swarms of birds. The survivor is most likely going to lose three of his limbs.

 

Josephus and Ty started talking about arranging prisoner transport to Waterdeep. Josephus felt it was a waste and they should just kill the prisoner. Ty felt this was unethical as he is unarmed and bound. This debate went on for a little while. Armond and Barikhol went into meditation.

 

The heroes heard a stir from the prisoners room. It appears the prisoner managed to kill himself with his chains.

 

The heroes traveled south and on the way to Feathergale Spire , they stopped by The Vale of Dancing Waters . The dwarven shrine would not allow entry, but did offer a hut outside for the travelers to stay in.

 

The next morning, the heroes made their way to Feathergale.

 

(There was a lot of role-play this adventure. It was awesome, thank you all. I feel there was really good ethical discussion with Josephus and Ty about killing the hostage. After the game Chris, Rick, and Shane spoke about how their characters would continue this ethical discussion on the journey. They probably talked about other things like torturing prisoners by making them write checks to their water utilities.)

 

Everyone leveled.
