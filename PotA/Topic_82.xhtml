Game Day

TLDR

  • The beginning of the story started with the heroes arriving at the Vale of Dancing Waters.
  • They learned about movement of the 4 Cults in Beliard and near IronForge.
  • The interrogated Larrakh to learn more about Aerisi, the weapon Ironfang, and where the stone armor the Orc were wearing came from.
  • Barikhol killed Larrakh. Armond and Barikhol got into an argument about this.
  • At Red Larch the heroes reconnected with everyone.
  • They got information where the Cult of the Crushing Wave are.
  • The Lord of Lance Rocks’ father Imuesk came asking for the Dagger of Velsharoon. Armond asked him to join them to get it back.

Detailed Entry

 Our adventure started off with the heroes arriving at the Vale of Dancing Waters. Once they arrived the Mud-Bloods informed the heroes they need to head to Waterdeep. Joclyn was to meet her contact Father Franz. Tyelknowle asked if they would be ok with him joining them.

 

The rest of the heroes knew they needed to heal up and it was going to take a while. Barikhol and Grimble went into the Vale of Dancing Waters and Barikhol started tending to the sick. He worked for a while, but became exhausted and had to go back out to rest.

 

Rowena went away from the rest of the survivors to meditate. She found herself in the High Forest, but it was many years ago. She was a child running through the woods. She was chasing after something. She got to a place in the forest and she could no longer see the light from the sun. Looking up at the canopy she could see that it was still day, but the light wasn’t making its way down to her.

 

She moved further and eventually found the thing she was chasing. It was a fox, no… it was her fox. Rowena’s spirit animal. It is hurt and it was licking its wounds. Rowena sat on the ground and the fox slowly came to her and crawled up in her arms. If she didn’t know any better she would think it felt sick. It was shivering, but it went to sleep.

 

Armond tracked down one of the prisoners. He was a dwarf. Armond asked him, “You don’t happen to know Brother Eardon do you?”

 

The Dwarf looked a little suspicious at Armond and answered, “Well, yes. I do. Why do you?”

 

Armond told him how Brother Eardon was concerned and asked Armond to look into finding the Mirabar Delegation. Armond explained all he knew about the delegation and asked the dwarf if there was anything he saw.

 

The Dwarf nodded and explained, “We were being chased by something large. It was some kind of burrowing creature. We sent the delegation ahead while four of us stood back to take care of it. We fought it and we lost. One of my brothers in arms died while fighting. The rest of us were captured and taken in. I hoped that the Mirabar Delegation got away, but it appears they did not.”

 

The Dwarf explained there were 35-40 of them. Armond only remembers seeing roughly 15 or so on the ground where the wreckage was located.

 

Barikhol and Grimble returned from the Vale of Dancing Waters to find Armond and some others around a campfire. It was a little quiet, but this is probably due to people being locked up for months and some of these people, longer.

 

Halli made his way down to the camp to meet with the heroes. After he sits down he says “It has been a while since I have heard anything about you all. Some rumored that the Heroes of Red Larch may be dead.”

 

The heroes explained what has happened to them for the last half a year. How the cults had captured them and put them into slave labor. Halli seems to grow dark at the mention of the cults.

 

“Their activity has grown. You hear about them more and more these days. Down south there has been battles between the Air and Water cults near Ironforge. I am not sure the Bargewright Inn has been repaired as of yet. In the north, the fire cult terrorized the town of Beliard. Some monks of ours have been up there tending to the wounded and helping rebuild. Stories from all over of children going missing have been making its rounds.” Halli says.

 

After his stories, some food, and telling everyone goodnight, Halli headed back to the Vale of Dancing Waters and left the travelers to get some rest. After everyone was a sleep Armond, with Barikhol and Grimble, grabbed Larrakh and dragged him away from the others.  

 

They started threatening him, Barikhol and Grimble took turns punching him. Armond finally settled them.

 

Armond told Larrakh “This is what I want to know. I want to know who Queen Aerisi is. I want to know what Ironfang is. I can make your death quick or I could drag you all the way back to Red Larch.”

 

Larrakh replied, “I’m used to pain. I have answers for your questions, and I know what you want. So this is what I want. I want to be able to walk away from here. Do we have a deal?”

 

Armond said “If I let you leave, you leave the Sword Coast and never return. You don’t hurt anyone on the way out.”

 

Barikhol and Grimble spoke up, “We are not good with that. Larrakh isn’t going to live.”

 

Armond, Barikhol, and Grimble got into an argument about needing this information. Armond made the point that if they want to do anything about these cults they need to know this, but Barikhol and Grimble didn’t budge. Armond threw his hands up and said, “Then this is a waste of time.” Then he walked away.

 

Barikhol and Grimble talked and Barikhol told Grimble, “I give you my word, he is not going to harm anyone ever again. Leave me with him.”

 

Barikhol started negotiating with Larrakh. He told him that he will honor Armonds agreement and if he gives him the information including information about the Orcs with stone armor he will let him live.

Larrakh was kind of surprised by this. Larrakh chose his words of “Let me walk out of here” thinking they would never let him live. So Larrakh knew he was going to have to fight for his life, but Barikhol was offering to let him live.

 

So Larrakh told him everything he wanted to know:

“Aerisi is no queen. Sure she leads the Air Cult, but if she is calling herself Queen she must be power hungry.” Larrakh takes a moment to think. “Aerisi is an elf. Ever since she was a little girl she wanted to fly. It became an obsession to her. This is how Yan-C-Bin, her god, found her. Yan-C-Bin taught her how to grow wings and Aerisi learned the ways of air.”

 

{Forgive me, I couldn’t remember the order of your questions and some of the actual questions themselves. I should have taken a break and typed some notes.}

 

Barikhol asked, “The Feathergale Knights, are they her followers?”

 

Larrakh looked to Barikhol and said, “Aerisi does have some followers, but this is outside the scope of our agreem...”

 

Barikhol said, “Fine… Fine.” Cutting Larrakh off. “What can you tell me about the knife, Ironfang?”

 

Larrakh instinct is to protect the Cult of the Black Earth at all cost, but over the last five months he has grown to think these heroes may just have what it takes to bring the Cult down. He takes a second to decide and says, “Ironfang is not a knife. It is a war pick. A war pick infused with the power of Ogremoch. Long ago the Elemental Evil connected with a legendary weapons smith here in this world. It helped him create the four weapons of the elements. Marlos Ur-nrayle was the one to find Ironfag and his devotion to Ogremoch helped those two come together.”

 

Barikhol asked “What are the other 3 weapons?”

 

Larrakh replied “I’m sorry, that I will not tell you for that was not part of our deal.”

 

Barikhol replied, “Fine. Stone Armor with the Orcs. Go.”

 

Larrakh had to think back. He knew he remembered something about stone armor, but wasn’t sure specifically of the context of the Orcs. So he told Barikhol what he remembered and hopefully that will be enough. “The creatures of less nature were first. The god of evil earth took creatures like Orcs, Trolls, Goblins…”

 

Barikhol interrupted, “Kobolds?”

 

Larrakh looked discussed, “gods no. No one wants to work with kobolds. Anyway Ogremoch created from the earth mystical armor. The armor has been passed from generation to generation. The creatures work for the Cult of the Black Earth now. Ogremoch has blessed his armor to others in the cult, but not many ask for it.”

 

Larrakh thinks if he knows anything else. He doesn’t want to be caught on a technicality. “I believe I have fulfilled the word of our agreement. Has my information been acceptable?”

 

Barikhol nods, “Yeah… just not enough for me.” and then punches Larrakh in the throat. It was a single strike, but it was laid perfectly. Barikhol could see the surprise on Larrakh’s face, but he could also see the realization that Larrakh knew he was about to die. Larrakh smiled, tried to laugh, but choked and blood came up.

 

As Larrakh was laying there dying he was angry that he was tricked, but proud that he caused a monk to lose his control and discipline. Hopefully this action will cause Barikhol to one day take up power that Larrakh was releasing into the world. After lots of coughing of blood and convulsing, Larrakh finally laid quiet.

 

Barikhol picked him up and walked him to the river. After disposing of the body he made his way back to camp.

 

Once he met up with Armond and Grimble he went over everything he learned from Larrakh. Armond asked “So when does Larrakh leave?”

 

Barikhol said, “He is not leaving. I killed him.”

 

This brought Armond and Barikhol into an argument. Armond was asking questions like “How can I trust you?” “Why would you do something like this?” Barikhol took all the hits and would explain, “It is a burden I will have to carry.” Grimble made the argument that “Barikhol didn’t lie to us. He could have told you he let him go, but he didn’t.”

 

The argument continued as they do. Armond is having a real tough time with this. Unknown directly to the others, Armond looked to Barikhol for guidance. Armond and Barikhol quickly became friends because they have a similar past. They both have been through trials and tribulations that have been comparable. Armond sees a darkness in himself and he struggles every day to control it. Barikhol, being a monk of Ilmater, is a dwarf of upstanding virtue. If Barikhol can go through the things that Armond has gone though and continue to hold on to his integrity, Armond feels that he has a chance. This action is something that Armond can see himself doing, but hopes he doesn’t.

 

The Heroes found that the Vail of Dancing Waters has monks that will be heading north and some that will be heading south. The heroes are free to travel with them if they would like. They decided that they would like to go back to Red Larch.

 

Once they arrived in Red Larch, it had that feeling of coming back home. They returned the Wagon and Horse to Stannor. Armond went to see Harburk, Grimble went to the Helm at Highsun, and Barikhol went to go check on his 9 Hammers and a Keg Brewery.

 

On his way to the 9 Hammers and a Keg Brewery <It needs a name> Barikhol saw out of the corner of his eye someone about to jump him. He got into ready position, but at last second he noticed it was Grund. Grund picked him up into a big ol’ orc hug. Grund told Barikhol all about the happenings around town. He talked about how he became deputy, he even puffed out his chest to show his star. Grund was really excited to go tell everyone they are back, when he ran off Barikhol could see the kids pillaging his “Orc Fingers” cart.

When Barikhol entered he saw a person inside his 9 Hammers and a Keg Brewery. After 5 months being away, this was the first thing Barikhol wanted to come back to. Coming back he finds that an intruder is here in his space. This is HIS space! He controls this place. Not someone else. Barikhol started concentrating to regain control. Barikhol is a monk and he shouldn’t be feeling these kinds of feeling about possessions.

 

Barikhol closes his eyes and allows his other senses to help center him. When he opens his eyes again he is staring into the eye of Grinarv. Barikhol was not sure he had enough meditation practice to prepare him for this.

 

“Barikhol my old friend. You’re back!” Grinarv said hobbling up to him. “You like it? Grinarv tried to keep it up to your tastes. Here! Here! Come take a look.” Grinarv said as he was trying to show Barikhol around the place. This seemed a bit unlike what Barikol remembers of him. He noticed Grinarv kept the place up to his specs. There were a couple pieces of equipment that failed and Grinarv repaired them. He actually did a pretty good job. He seems to know something about this trade.

 

Barikol talked him through a couple of things he noticed around the 9 Hammers and a Keg Brewery that Grinarv could do better. Grinarv was grateful for the feedback. Barikhol grabbed Grinarv by the shoulder and told him. “Grinarv, I misjudged you. You have done a really good job here.”

 

“Barikhol, you were right to judge me. As children Grinarv was an asshole. Grinarv remembers what Grinarv and friends did and said about you and your father.” Grinarv said.. of course in third person. “Your father would have been proud.”

 

Barikhol nodded, “That means a lot. Thank you.” He took a moment to pause and look around at the place. “It looks like you are going to be on staff, let’s workout finances I can’t think of anyone better I would like to have run the place while I am away.”

 

Grimble was in the Helm at Highsun. He was seeing all the towns’ people. People of different races and he could tell different backgrounds. This town doesn’t seem like everyone was born here. It makes sense, this is a travel through town. A large man with a chipped tooth and three golden earring approaches the table. “Welcome traveler to the Helm at Highsun. I am Justran, what can I get you?”

 

Grimble orders some food and ale, and continues watching the people while waiting on his new found acquaintances.

Harburk was chopping poultry going about his business. Harburk has been extremely busy for the last few months. After the mess with Believers , the town got really suspicious of its community. This was very difficult for Harburk combat. Harburk is not very political. He is fair and just. He can enforce order, but being persuasive is just not in his tool belt. He also had to travel south to Waterdeep for the trial of Ilmeth and Elak . This was tough on the soul. He knew these two for many years. They weren’t killers, they just believed in the wrong man, a monster, and made a terrible mistake, one they could not take back. The weight of their execution weighed heavy on him.

Harburk was deep in thought when the door ringed. A man entered and Harburk said his standard “I’ll be with you in one…” But he didn’t finish. Harburk glanced up to expect to see a townsfolk or a traveler. He never could bring himself to say it, but he thought the Heroes of Red Larch may never return.

 

Harburk cleaned himself up and walked over to shake Armond’s hand. “Welcome home. It sure is good to see you.” He said.

 

They caught up, Harburk shared how there was a betting pool if you all were still alive. Explained that Wyer has been taking care of your affairs since you have been gone. Kaylessa will be happy to hear that the room will be used again. Harburk then offered to cover their meals at the Helm at Highsun.

Rowena came back to the town and wasn’t sure where to go. She went over to the Haeleeya’s Bathhouse to clean up. When she walked in Haeleeya stood up and came running up to her. “the gods have listened, you have returned!” Haeleeya said.

 

Haeleeya took a quick look at Rowena and could see that her wounds go deeper than just her skin. “it’s ok, you’re safe now.” Haeleeya didn’t know the words to say to help Rowena , and didn’t know if the words existed.

 

Rowena had not been touched by another human for the last 5 months in a kind way. She wasn’t sure how to process this. Rowena hasn’t had a good year. She joined an adventure party who all but one was slaughtered by zombies. She was captured by the necromancer who killed Odert and placed his soul inside her. She began life with this new adventure party, and they too were captured. She was tortured for 5 months, and now she thinks her spirit fox is dying. Processing this is not an option and Rowena immediately hugs Haeleeya again.

 

Haeleeya glances over at one of her employees and moves her eyes to indicate for her to prep a bath. Haeleeya let’s Rowena hold on to her for as long as she needs.

 

The scene cuts and Rowena is taking a long overdue bath. Haeleeya’s younger sister Lippa, who drew Rowena the bath, checks up on her “Is everything to expectations? May I fetch you something to drink?” When Lippa doesn’t hear a reply she glances over to see Rowena trying to answer, but bubbles coming out of her mouth. The employee get nervous as Rowena begins to levitate in a bubble. The bubble floats up to the ceiling and pops and Rowena splashes back down in the bath water.

 

When she comes crawling out of the bath she looks up at the employee and says “I’m ok.” The employee screams and runs out of the bathhouse. Rowena thought for a second and realized she didn’t say “I’m ok.” She actually didn’t say anything at all. She made some noises, but not words.

 

Rowena shook, to dry herself and then crawled over to the mirror. “Bark” (translation: “Well Shit”). In the mirror staring back at her is a fox. Rowena saw out the door what looked to be her spirit fox. She decided to follow him.

Out of town they ran, Rowena could visibly see that her spirit fox wasn’t 100%. He was still wounded, still sick. They ran and ran. While on their little adventure, they ran into a… well… Rowena didn’t know what it was. But the thing that could only be described as darkness attacked them. During the fight, Rowena allowed her Wild Magic to spark and could see the stress it caused her Spirit after each burst.

 

Once Rowena made it home… well snuck back to the bathhouse to retrieve her cloths, then home. She had a lot to think about. On her way to her room, she could feel a wild surge. Rowena suppressed it the best she could and went to her room to get some rest.

{/Cut Scene}

 

The remainder of the week went pretty smooth. The heroes met with different people around town. There was a moment, where the town paid their respects to Josephus. Everyone in the Helm at Highsun took a piece of crumble bread and the room went around telling stories. Everyone took a bite and chewed… and chewed… and chewed some more Marielye, the bar maid, started laughing first. Then a couple other people. Then eventually the whole room started letting out a gut laugh.

 

A caravan came rolling through town. It is a known group. They travel in a circle from Mirabar to Waterdeep, then up the coast to Luskan, last back to Mirabar, and then back again. Travelers from all over visited the stores and shops. It was a good day for Red Larch.

 

That evening two beautiful women entered the Helm at Highsun. After visiting for a little bit one of the women said “I am looking to hire someone. My ship was pirated by some assholes flying this symbol (She holds up the symbol of the Cult of the Crushing Wave). Must we travel all the way to Waterdeep for help?”

 

Barikhol was quick to stand up and greet the ladies. “Me lases, have uh seat, let’s tulk.” The red head looked over to the blonde to see what she thought. The blonde took a look at the table and looked back and shrugged.

 

They approached the table and sat down. They introduced themselves at Teria and Sesa. They explained their ship was stolen on the Dessarin River. The heroes had to promise to return half the cargo from the ship, plus the ship, and they can keep the other half as payment. Once agreed they asked “Do you really think you can handle this job?”

 

Barikhol replied “How about give us a night to think it over, and you go and talk to the town’s people about what we can do.”

 

Teria said “We seal a deal a little differently where we are from.” Sesa waves down Marielye. When she comes over Sesa whispers something in her ear, Marielye nods, and goes back to retrieve shots. “This is from my family’s distillery.”

 

Everyone shot it back, and it hit them hard. It was probably, if not the best, one of the best whiskeys everyone ever tasted. Barikhol looked up at Teria and asked “I think you may want to try something from my 9 Hammers and a Keg Brewery.”

The heroes had fun that night. Everyone was drinking, music was playing, people were dancing, Barikhol and Teria may have snuck off at some point, and everyone had legitimate fun. For a splitting moment one could forget that the lands were plagued with evil occultist kidnapping people into slavery, killing merchants for loot, robbing and burning villages, and more. The heroes deserved this moment. They do not get too many of these. So they lived this like it was their last. Because, well you never know.

 

The next morning the heroes were feeling refreshed and enjoying their breakfast (at roughly 10:00AM). They agreed to Teria and Sesa to get their ship back. The party agreed they needed money and this was a good job. The bonus was it was also part of the mission they set off on. Teria and Sesa decided not to travel on to Waterdeep.

 

While they were finishing their meal a boy known as Braelen walks in. “Armond?” he asks approaching the table.

 

Armond turned to the child and remembered him. “Braelen, what can I do for you?”

 

“Armond there is a man on the south end of town wanting to speak with you.” He replied. The boy looked nervous and scurried off.

 

The heroes all took a glance at each other, shrugged, grabbed their stuff, and headed to the edge of town.

 

Harburk was standing there with his arms crossed looking at the visitor standing about 200 feet from the edge of town. They had to speak up to hear one another, but their voices carried well enough. “Are you Armond of the Heroes of Red Larch?” he says. Now that everyone has gotten a closer look, this man is standing with two creatures, which appear to be zombies, chained to him.

 

“I am.” Armond replied, “Who may you be?”

 

“My name is Imusek, father of Oreioth, he was also known as the Lord of Lance Rock. I believe you sent this.” He says holding up one of the flyers that Armond had Endrith put together.

 

“OK, you found me, what do you want?” Armond said to the necromancer.

 

“I come only asking for a possession he held that was never his to begin with. I also request to know where his body is.” Imusek replied.

 

Armond thinks and realizes he threw the body into the quarry. He is about to speak up when Harburk shouted “The body is buried south of here.” Harburk gives a landmark so the necromancer can find him. Armond looks over at Harburk and he gives Armond a little side smirk.

 

“Thank you. The item I am looking for is known as The Dagger of Velsharron. I would like that back please.” He says. The necromancer speaks with an annoying high voice, like his son.

 

“Unfortunately we do not have that item. We know who took it.” Armond replied.

 

“Will you retrieve it for me? That dagger in the wrong hands can do terrible things.” Imusek said.

 

“Like what?” Grimble asked.

 

“You don’t question the great Imusek, I shall rip…” Imusek thought, but said “The dagger holds many properties. It can summon the soul of long ago and place it in a young man’s body.”

 

The heroes didn’t seemed to be phased by this idea. Imusek thought that a man of the cloth would have a great problem with this. Souls should move on, to take death away from someone should be cruel.

 

“How about this.” Armond said, “You help us get it back and it’s yours.”

 

“Like hell I will travel with you filthy lot…” Imusek thought, but decided to say “Surely your paladin may have a problem with this.”

 

“I don’t want to travel with you either, but if it is for the greater good, I will consider it.” Grimble said.

 

“Interesting.” Imusek thought. “I shall think about it, I will need a tenday. I will return to this spot.”

 

Imusek walked away.
