Game Notes

Day Two - Date: unknown (still); Location: White Ridge

I am still not clear on my location. I do know that the closest village is called White Ridge . A rough and tumble lumber town. There was only one location for us to find a place to stay in. I was able to find lodging for myself, So, and Gae. However, I am getting ahead of myself.

 

On the road to this village, we encountered an ambush. A broken wagon blocked the road and orcs laid in wait to the side. Fortunately we had the honorable Kami, Princess Autumn . She was able to determine by simply observing the birds that trouble laid before. She ordered a stop to the caravan and the heroes I traveled with bravely went forth. I was honored to follow them and witness their skills. Foolishly, I moved into the woods but Vyn and Luca moved in to point out the ambush and saved me.

 

It was amazing to watch them work. Vyx was shouting and singing and even though we faced ferocious orcs , I couldn’t help but smile at the confidence she portrayed. I was very nervous and thus fumbled around before the lessons of my sifu took over. Luca was very quick to move and engage with the obvious leader. I’m glad he was there. An orc up in a tree clobbered me with some javelins and Vyn was there in an instant to heal me. I must confess that his blessing, “May you hold to Death” was a little unnerving, but his blessings were much appreciated. It was fascinating to watch him and Vyx simply order the spirits to cast the orcs own weapons against them. I was very impressed with the Kami whose bird spirit flashed all across the battlefield even while she called upon the powers of ice and frost, freezing the enemy where they stood.

 

All the violence clashing around me was most distressing. Even more so, when the orcs were mortally wounded. Their eyes would wash over in blood and they would simply ignore their wounds even as their life blood spilled out all around them and on us. I tried my best to stand with the heroes but I found myself scrambling for my life. The fight ended with Vyx facing the leader with the Kami’s owl spirit. Both the orc leader and Vyx were struck down by each other. I’ve only read of such adventures. Seeing them is so much more scarier. Fortunately the Kami was able to tend to her wounds. Then, Vyn communed with the Kami of the lands (or perhaps the ancestors?). They restored us and thanked us for banishing the orcs .

 

After the fight, I was saddened to discover the body of a half-elf that had been murdered. We gave him a proper burial, Vyn is very wise when it comes to funerary rites. We also burned the orcs on a pyre.

 

All that to say, I was most relieved to have a bath when we got to the village.

 

I must also find a way to make better tea, if for no other reason than to thank the Heroes for keeping me and the entire caravan safe. I am unsure what to do now, but I am confident I will survive long enough to form a plan.

A vicious enemy is better countered by the cooperation of one’s allies rather than the steel in one’s shirt.
