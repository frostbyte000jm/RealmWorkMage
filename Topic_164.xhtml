Josephus Collaborative Adventure

Stories

Josephus, Wyer, Barikhol

Josephus , joined the army of a local warlord in search of adventure and glory.  Instead, he found himself pulling KP duty more often than not.  Not content to lead a military career peeling potatoes, he constantly worked to improve his martial prowess.  He worked tirelessly during his off-duty times to practice with anyone who was willing.  In addition to his military duties, he took time to learn the culinary arts so that he could provide his brothers-at-arms with the best camp food.  Because of his dedication and determination, he quickly advanced through the ranks of the kitchen staff and became the head chef.  He was deadly with a blade on and off the field and them men in the camp loved him.  One day, Josephus was preparing a delicious stew for his men.  Instead of grabbing the seasoning, he mistakenly grabbed some nightshade powder.  Many men died that day due to unknown causes.  Too late, Josephus realized his mistake.  He resigned from the military life and ventured out on his own in search of redemption.  He told no one his mistake and vowed to never do so.

 

Wyer stumbled through the woods, dazed and bleeding. That was stupid. Attacking those kobolds alone was not a good idea . Why didn't Wyer just invite Armond and Josephus ? But he had been hunting them, his quarry, for weeks and was exhausted, out of supplies, and just plain frustrated with the time wasted on false trails and missed opportunities to attack. So had charged in like the idiot he sometimes thought he was. Now he was going to die hungry, alone and wounded in these god forsaken woods. The kobolds had apparently also been out of food. Now it three days later and Wyer knows he is going to die of starvation. He stubbornly trudges onward.

 

But wait…. what was that smell? Heavenly, impossible, delicious, Wyer followed his nose and finds a miracle, a small camp with a bumbling pot set over a small fire. Without the lack of fear that he should have for a strange camp in a strange land, with his last reserve of energy, he stumbles into the camp and collapses before the fire unconscious.

 

Barikhol trudged along, just leaving the Anuroch Desert with Armond .  It had been a long hard trek, and Barikhol and Armond had run out of rations and water, with only a small cask of blueberry wine left that he had brought from the temple.  Thankfully, he spied light from a campfire a short distance away.  Hopefully these were travelers, and not orcs or goblins, or kobolds, which were known to inhabit the area. Armond gave Barikhol directions and said he will be there shortly.

 

As Barikhol moved closer, he smelled a wonderful scent, possibly potato soup!  Knowing that no monster could cook something that smelled so delicious, Barikhol strode into the camp, hoping that he could trade some of his blueberry wine for supper.  Upon stepping into the firelight, the first thing that Barikhol saw was a large man in chain mail armed with a spoon standing over a bloody and beaten body!  Barikhol had seen the masters of his order use unusual weapons, but to do this to someone with a spoon was frightening!  The poor man on the ground had no chance!

 

Barikhol quickly stepped in front of the prone man, ready to defend him from the spoon wielding brute.  The brute backed away with a laugh, and quickly explained the situation.

 

Josephus , having explained the situation invited Barikhol to stay to eat.  During this time, Josephus and Barikhol eased Wyer into a sitting position and spooned stew into his mouth and gave him water.  Armond returned to see this situation and asked "what happened?" Eventually, Wyer came around enough to explain what he was doing in the area.
