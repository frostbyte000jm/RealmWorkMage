Game 28.0 (Last Two Years)

Game Day

Game Date: 06/16/18

Detailed Entry

The curse overwhelms Barikhol. He has haunting memories of days he couldn’t control it. Leokas and Barikhol travel far and wide looking for a cure. They tried many things, and had many failures.

 

Barikhol and Leokas learned about the battle against Bastain while they were away. When they came across a town they followed a fire cult group and found a war room. Barikhol and Leokas read over the map and fire battle plans and realized their side doesn’t stand a chance. That is when Barikhol had the idea, “use me, it’s a full moon”. Leokas was reluctant, but it looked like there wasn’t a better option.

 

Barikhol went back to the werecreatures camp and asked for them to follow him to the battle field. The weres did at once. On the way they ran and Barikhol could feel his internal instincts take over. Rage, desire, hope, protection, and fear ran through him. Once the moon was in view in the sky he started to change and so did everyone else. All that could be heard was growls and where there was running there is now galloping. Off in the distance the battle could be heard.

 

In the non-full moon cycles Barikhol somehow found time for his Brewery. With the help of Teria (Barikhol and Teria have spent a lot time together during these last two years), the 9 Hammers and a Keg Brewery has expanded. Grinarv has been a big help “Barikhol you can trust ol’ Grinarv. Grinarv knows how these businesses go and Grinarv will never let you down.”

 

In the expansion Barikhol along with Father Franz and a few members of the Mud-Bloods started an orphanage. The brewery generated revenue to help get these kids off the street, take care of them, and eventually get them rehoused, trained, and educated.

 

Leokas spent sleepless hours researching how to cure Barikhol. Many times his mind and body tried to tell him to give up, but Leokas prayed to Palor to give him the endurance to save his friend. He found in his travels that he started connecting more with Palor. Some things would just fall into place and it would leave Leokas wondering if it was coincidence. But they became more and more frequent and many times it turned out that if he wasn’t there people would have died or bad things would have happened.

 

Leokas remembers Wyer’s teaching on the theology. It finally started to make sense. Every now and then Leokas’ scar would bother him. Random things would happen like he would lose little bits of time or hear something off in the distance. Leokas may not have even related this to his scar.

 

There was a moment where it started making sense. He teamed up with some of the soldiers who rescued him after he was held prisoner. They were fighting against the earth cult. When out came Marlos. Leokas grabbed his friend and tried to pull him back, but he felt a pain in his chess that made him hesitate. If he reacted he may have been able to pull Nir out of the way before Marlos turned him to stone, but that jolt of pain stopped him.

 

Leokas dealing with the lost of his old friend and watching others dying at the hands of the earth cult had a moment of fear. That is when he heard Marlos. “We see you Leokas, we know who you are, we know what you and the Heroes of Red Larch are up to. We knew were you were going to be and that is why we are here. We control you. The Elder Eye sees all. You have lost.”

 

Leokas fought through the pain and the panic. After a quick prayer to Paylor he got whoever was left and they got out of there.

 

Two months later Leokas was woken up with a vision. He was standing in his home town. It was on fire. The Earth cult was marching through pulling people out of their homes and executing them on the streets. Leokas tried to wake up, but he couldn’t. He felt it, the scar, the elementals were showing him this vision.

 

Marlos dragged his family out onto the streets. “I know you two, and I know your son is watching.” Leokas’ mother and father were scared. “This is the price you have to pay for your son and his troop interfering with the elemental evil.” and he proceeded to turn them into stone. Then raised a Warhammer known as Ironfang and started to smash it down on his mother and father.

 

“NOOOOOO!!!!!!!!” a high pitched scream came from somewhere and Leokas felt the sting of something in his chest. Leo opens up his eyes and see Leaf punching him and zapping at his chest to snap Leokas out of it. “You have to get that under control! May master needs you to come to breakfast!” which was an odd thing to say, but leaf seemed to have his priorities sometimes askew when it came to food.

 

“Wait!” Leokas said, and Leaf hovered in front of him waiting. “Don’t tell Armond.”

 

“Not my secret, I can’t.” Leaf replied. “But your food is getting cold!”

 

Rowena exhausted the books she had for research and decided to travel to the elemental planes to learn more about these cults. What she learned is that the Elemental Evil lives in a world between the elements and it is a disease to the elemental planes. The more power it gains the more it will be able to take over the elemental planes. She also learned that the Orbs take a sacrifice to create. Life magic is very powerful and when a life is sacrificed it can create all sorts of disastrous things.

 

Rowena found she missed planes traveling. When she prepares to travel it is more about preparing herself. The heroes of Red Larch on a normal day would see Rowena doing weird things like running down the streets with her arms out feeling the wind, building sand castles, getting in to water bag fights, and playing with the town’s children “the floor is lava”.

 

While Rowena was on the Elemental Plane of Water Rowena found Elodin her twin brother working at, now that Rowena sees the place it is obvious that he would be here, the liquid library. “Elodin, my bother, it has been so long.”

 

“Too long, I go by Enna now.”

 

Rowena looks over… her sister Enna. “Enna, my sister, how have I missed you.” They hug like long lost syblings should and catch up.

 

Enna went on the final mission with their grandmother. There was an attack and a blast of magic and Enna woke up being rescued by merfolk getting her to a bubble (cities in the Elemental Plane of Water). Enna was never good at planes travel, that was always Ro’s talent, so she found what she was good at and started doing it.

 

Rowena asked Enna to come home with her, but Enna told Rowena “I want to, and I really hope to visit one day, but I have a family here.” She takes Rowena to her home down in the channels. “I want you to meet your niece, nephew, and brother in law.” Rowena spent a couple weeks with them while using the library to learn more about the orbs. In the end she went home without her sister, but they now know how to get ahold of each other.

 

Rowena in a fit of wild magic made two shells that have a pattern weaved in them that is similar to the ones she used on her bag of holding. They can contact each other by holding the shell and whispering their name.

 

Grimble made it his top priority to hunt and destroy orbs. Grimble would rush off to Dwarf villages to hunt down cult members. One day why visiting Mirabar Grimble found a statue, in the middle of town. It was the heroes of Red Larch. On the face his statue Grimble wore a mask. It was the mask of Gorm. The description on the statue had an inscription “In remembrance of the heroes that saved Mirabar in 1491”

 

Grimble had a memory of playing on these streets as a child. Grimble and his friend Amnom was playing a game Mosters and Heroes. Grimble was playing the hero dwarf hunting down Amnom the evil elf. They played with sticks and cookware for their swords and shields. Amnom had this move where he would slide past Grimble and catch him in a random leg and flip him over, spinning up to have his wooden spoon right in front of Grimble. “Got you now!” They had a lot of fun playing together.

 

Grimble was in Waterdeep waiting on a messenger from the Feathergale Knights when a Water Cult Raid happened. The city was hit with a devastation orb. Rain, floods, whirl pools, and more attacked the streets. The cultist ran through the streets attacking civilians and government buildings. Grimble started fighting back. Grimble watched one of the cultist surf up to the top of a shop. They sold the best whiskey in Waterdeep. Grimble grabbed onto his shield and let the current grab hold of him and push his shield up towards the top of the building, pulling Grimble behind.

 

The fight was down to who was more skilled Grimble with his axe VS the watercult fighter with his hammer. The water cultist was a dwarf. Grimble yelled “Why did you forsake the Dwarf gods? Your kind is killing so many innocents.”

 

“HA!” the cultist mocked “The dwarf gods are small, weak, they couldn’t save Raven Rock, they couldn’t save Katwaen.”

 

Grimble thought to himself “where have I heard that name?” he realized he took his eyes off the cultist for a split second, and before he knew it the watercult dwarf slid by him flipping Grimble onto his back. Grimble saw the hammer just above his face.

 

On instinct Grimble said “Amnom?” We see Grimble swing his foot and then Grimble as a child doing the same thing. One foot goes over the elbow and the other kicks the hammer swinging it back up to the attackers face and pulling Grimble up from the ground.

 

The watercult attacker said outloud “Grimble?” as Grimble took the cult member down and kicked off his helmet. Grimble had his axe raised high and Amnom looked into his face and saw the face of Gorm. Amnom begun to cry and beg. Grimble brought his axe down and crushed the helmet.

 

“Go.” Grimble said “Go home. Just… go home.” Amnom got up and ran.
