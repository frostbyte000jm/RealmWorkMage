The heroes go running out of monastery. Tyelknowle spills something on the floor as he is leaving and Joclyn is seen rolling her eyes. The heroes, Mud-Bloods , and seven slaves go running down the path towards the getaway wagon.

Ander and Tyelknowle Cover the rear. Joclyn and Grimble protect the slaves. Otho is giving help to Armond , Barikhol , and Rowena who are injured and without their normal gear.

Making it a little further down the hill the heroes were meet with a mob of guards and earth sorcerers.

 

{For the people who are actually interested how this works: Minion rules were established back in DnD1e. In DnD4e the developers thought it would be cool to reintroduce the idea of minions (apparently there was some extended rules in 3.5e that used minions too.). Unfortunately, even tho DnD4e had a lot of really cool attributes like this one, they missed the feel of DnD for many players. A lot of the cool things that are in 4e made it to 5e, but unfortunately this little gem missed the transition. Many players have toyed with bringing it over and the developers have talked about releasing a 5e version of the rule in an upcoming campaign book.

 

Minion rules for this game works as such: Instead of throwing a bunch of 1/8 CR creatures at the heroes, and the heroes taking down 1 bad guy at a time, you change it up to a little higher CR and record 1 stat for a group of creatures. Each group has 3 - 5 (or could be more) creatures in it. They act as a group and die as a group. So there is only one HP stat and one attack stat between all of them.

 

When the minion attacks they get a +X to their attack and damage. X = number in the mob – 1. So a mob with 4 people gain a +3 to attack and damage. When the Mob is damaged the mob is reduced by members based on how many hit points they have. So 20 HP with a 4-person mob has 5 HP each. When the mob is reduced their attacks and damage are reduced. So taking a 4-person mob (+3 attack/damage) to 2-person mob gives them (+1 attack/damage).

 

This helps the GM not have to keep track of SOOOOO many NPCs AND this allows the players to have those badass movie moments. I will not be using Minions all the time but when/if I do I will be very specific on who is a minion. }

At one point some Earth Mages caused a rock slide and this almost killed the party. This did kill many of the Earth Guards.
