Backstory

As a young child of peasant serfs, life was not all fun and games. From the time that Armond could walk, he was working the fields helping out his folks. Most of the week he tended to the lord's fields; plowing, seeding, fixing fences, or anything to keep the young serf busy. The other couple of days he attend his family's meager plot of land to provide food for his family and produce to sell at the market. One day while clearing a section of field of rocks, a young lord was passing by and noticed that Armond was diligently removing rocks while other children played. This dedication to his work at such a young age impressed the young lord. Lord Arthuris DeLark decide to pay the young serf's parents for their loss as Armond was now to serve as the lord's page/stable boy. Lord Arthuris gave Armond the rest of the day to get what little belongs he owned and say his goodbyes as he most likely would never see his parents again.

 Now as a page/stable boy Armond duties were no less tedious than he had been before, but just a different skill set. His main duties were to clean the horse stalls, maintain the horses, and help the master carpenter make repairs. Several years had pass and Armond had grown into a strong lad from all the manual labor. During his free time he would watch the house guard train and every once in a while Squire Fenner would partake in the exercises. When no one was looking Armond would go behind the stables and practice what he saw. Unbeknownst to Armond , Lord Arthuris had seen him several times practice what he could remember. One day as Armond was in the middle of his routine he was startled by Arthuris's approach, having seen Armond master the basic moves that he had witness, Lord Arthuris asked if he would like to formal train with the guard. As Armond eagerly accepted the lord's offer Lord Arthuris informed him that his duties came first and foremost and any slack in duties would result in disciplinary actions. Well Armond never slack in duties nor training. His dedication to training had afforded him with the prowess to best any man other than the swordmaster, Fenner, or Arthuris; for they had studied noble swordsmanship.

 During his time at Lord Arthuris's keep Armond had befriend many but no more so than Fenner. Fenner was the nephew of Arthuris and had been sent to become an apprentice squire under Arthuris in order to one day become a Knight. Fenner had all the makings to become a great knight. He easily understood social and political ethos, master of several languages, and was on his way to becoming a master swordsman. A little time after Armond join the guard training sessions, Fenner started taking a liking to him. Seeing a lot of himself in Armond , he decided to teach Armond the fine points of a noble's education; teaching him of military tactics and strategy as well as the noble sword play. As time went by the two of them grew close as brothers. When Armond was around seventeen years of age, the lord informed him that some of the guard, Fenner and Armond himself would be leaving to join the King's Purple Dragons for the King had taxed his vassals with orders for soldiers. Lord Autheris expressed his fondness for Armond and has come to regard him as a member of his family. Within the week the soldiers, including Armond and Fenner, left for the Capital.

  On the road to the Capital Fenner and Armond ran into a little trouble. While passing through a small village they saw a fire. As they rushed up to lend aid, they saw the village was under attack by goblins. They jumped into action and fought off these vile creatures, but unfortunately Armond and Fenner were too late. As they turned a corner the heroes notice a wagon was rushing away driven by a person that could have been human with dark red skin. There were two goblins looking back at the village and Armond could make out maybe 3 bodies in the back. The wagon was too far away for Armond and Fenner to catchup so they did all they could by helping to put out the flames.

While they were burning the bodies of the goblins Armond noticed that one of them had a branding on his upper leg that was covered with cloth before. It looks like a bowl and it looked new.

The village thanked the heroes and gave them food, drink, and a place to sleep for the night before they were on their way again the next day.

Upon arriving in the Capital, Suzail, the new recruits had to go through a rigorous evaluation to determine their skills. All of Lord Arturis's troop attain the highest level thanks in part to their master swordsman's teachings. Fenner and Armond advanced through the ranks in a relatively quick time with Fenner earning the rank of Swordcaptian (Lieutenant) and Armond Swordmajor (Sergeant Major) within 5-6 years. The higher ups took notice and assigned them into the Elite Guard. As elite guards their duty was to escort visiting nobles through the kingdom of Cormyr.

One fateful day while traveling from the border of the Dalelands south along the Stonelands to Gnoll's pass, the noble's caravan was ambushed by a gang of ogres (The Teeth, mercenaries). These ogres are different than the types read about in books. Most ogres couldn't be bothered with fine weapons and armor, but some of these ogres had well-constructed weapons and armor made of stone. It seemed to be only given to the 'leaders' of the ogre group (for lack of a better term). The group held their own, as well as any monster hunter could, but as the heat of battle raged on, Armond 's foolhardiness got the best of him and he, disobeying orders, charged after the fleeing ogres leaving very few soldiers to guard the caravan. When Armond came to his senses and hurried back he was horrified by the sight. A small second wave of ogres had attack the caravan from behind and slaughter the remaining soldiers. Only Fenner had managed to kill and fend them off until the war-chief torn through him with his great spear as Armond approached. Smiling with glee as he backed off, the war-chief had his men ignite the royal carriage ablaze and cause a landslide that pushed the royal carriage down the hill and mostly buried it as they scampered off with the treasure of the noble family on the other side where the survivors of the attack could not reach . Armond could hear screams of agony from within the carriage and fought through the flames to get to them but there was nothing that could be done, the flames were too intense. After he gave up trying to help the noble family Armond ran to Fenner side. As Fenner lay dying in his arms, Armond promised always to think first before acting as not to ever cause others such pain.

After Armond and the remaining soldiers of the company returned back to the garrison and Armond gave a full report of what happen; after words the wounded got medical care including Armond , for the fire had burned if left arm and back. Some days later Armond was call to the Constal (Colonel) for debriefing, During the debrief, Armond was informed that due to his actions he would be stripped of all rank, be administered administrative punishment, all land/wealth seized to pay restitution for the lives lost then exile from within the borders of Cormyr. As soon as the debriefing was over they took Armond to the stocks were they stripped him of his armor and administered 15 lashings. While still bleed and half conscious, they shacked him and loaded him in to a wagon and shipped him out of the realm to the city Iriaebor. Once they neared the city they un-sacked Armond and through him out of the wagon into the dirt. With no money, no food, and no shelter Armond slowly and drudgingly made his way to Iriaebor.
