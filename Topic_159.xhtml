Downtimes 09

 

While the group was investigating the injured survivor of the delegation Armond felt a pull to his idol. Armond sat down and started concentrating. Deep in his pocket he felt something get pulled out of him. Armond was falling. It was surreal, but looking around at his surroundings he was falling from himself. It was as if he was a miniature version of himself and he just fell from the height of his shoulder.

 

Maybe falling is the right word. He was being pulled. Like going down a mountain on a slide. Armond got jerked towards his belongs and saw that he was going to crash with his idol. Once Armond opened his eyes, he was laying on the floor of a cavern. It looked familiar, but he couldn't place it.

 

A child was standing over him looking down into Armonds face. The child was wearing a burlap sack and had a shaved head. "That was quite an entrance." the boy said while holding back a giggle. He offers a hand to bring Armond to his feet.

 

After Armond stands he asks "Where am I."

 

The boy replies "Where you were. This place has a name, but it isn't important and you don't want to know it. Let's call this place the in-between."

 

"Who are you." Armond asks.

 

The boy replies, "You know the answer to that question. For I called you and you and I are connected." The boy goes over to the small fire that lights the area and stokes it. "It has been a while since we have talked. Glad I finally got your attention. I tried to communicate with you when you were in the cave under Red Larch . Your friend has their magic. She can speak with the earth. I can feel what she does, but I can’t see it. I think you may be able to find a clue in her magic for your quest."

 

The boy starts drawing something in the ground with his scorched stick. "It is time I ask you for a favor. You are to kill your hostage. If he goes free he will alert the air cult you're on to them. He can't simply be killed. He needs to have his soul trapped."

 

"My friends are not going to like this." Armond says.

 

"They won't know you left the room. I will grant you access to Major Image and Invisibility. I will hold your Major Image once you leave the room." the boy says with a smile. The drawing is starting to look like a bird. "When you kill him, place the idol by his left foot. Energy leaves the body on the left side. I will capture him."

 

"You may deny this request, but I get two more before you can't deny me. The air genasi is going to die anyway." The boy tell you. "Do you accept this request?"

Finnis

Growing up is never easy, but when you don’t know who your father was and being the son to a whore always makes it a little harder. In the pass Finnis's mother was a beautiful young woman that courted many men of high standards. One day, as she was escorting a wealth merchant to a gala to celebrate the trade agreement with the Eldanians (Elves), she caught the eye of Lord Ailmar Faeranduil, Royal Ambassador of Eldania. Lord Ailmar was infatuated with this human beauty. Later that evening Ailmar stole away Theresa (Finnis's mother), dancing and chatting the night away. While Ailmar was in Irieabor, the two spent every waking moment with each other. Theresa was in heaven, hoping finally she found the right man to take her away. One the eve of Ailmar departure, Ailmar confessed his undying love for Theresa and consummated their relationship that night. In the morning Ailmar had Theresa go gather her belongings from her home. When she return the palace guards halted her from entering the royal guest quarters. She pleaded with them to let her in, telling them to go get Lord Ailmar to explain who she was but the guards only informed her that Lord Ailmar had already left. Devastated, Theresa slunk back to her megger home. Weeks later she found she was with child. Now with a growing midsection none of her wealth clientele wanted anything to do with her. Without their money she had to move into the slums where conditions where inhuman at times. The only bit of hope was Father Yorgenson, a cleric in the service of Ilmater. He would bring her food and provisions and what little coin he could scrap together. It was Father Yorgenson that delivered Theresa child Finnis. When Theresa saw her child she immediately became scornful of him, for he bared the look of the elves. Father Yorgenson told her not to hate her child for he had done nothing to her; but merely been born. Over the next few years all Theresa could do to earn a living was to turn cheap tricks for change. In doing so had caught a devastating disease that ravaged her body. Father Yorgenson tried everything he could but Theresa surcomed to the disease leaving behind her four year old son Finnis.

Father Yorgenson had sent Finnis to go live at the orphanage but kept an eye out for him. Finnis had a hard time relating to the other kids because he was different; his ears where pointer, his eyes slanted more and the fact he could see in the dark. The kids would often call him names like mudblood or half breed to name a few. They would also bully him and take is food and rip his clothes. So Finnis would hide away from the other kids, stealing food from the kitchen after it closed. Finnis started sneaking away from the orphanage during the day. One day while away from the orphanage Finnis ran into a group of homeless kids. They ask where he was from because they haven't seen him around before. He explained that he was from the orphanage but hate being there because of the treatment he received for being different. The group of kids let him join them telling him that the difference between them is what makes them great. As Finnis took a closer look at these kids he noticed that most of them weren't human. There were half elves, a half orc, and even a tiefling. Finnis asked, how they ate and where did they sleep? They answer that they make do by stealing what they need and sleep here and there. They invite Finnis to abandon the orphanage and join their group. After think long about it he decide to do just that. Over the next few months Finnis picked up the art of stealing and learned all the nooks and crannies throughout the city. Many a days were spent picking the pockets of travelers stopping to watch the entertainment in the square. One particular day while enjoying his favorite pastime he notice a man watching him. Finnis tried to sneak away but every time he did the man would appear in front of him. Scared, he ran as fast as he could down the alleys twisting and turn but was caught. The man took him aside and informed him that he has come to the attention of the thieves’ guild. He explain that thieving without the permission would get you punished. The man told Finnis that the guild has offered him a provisionary membership and with it he and his friends could pilfer without fear of retaliation from the guild with the standard payment of 50% of the plunder. Thinking of his friend’s safety he accepted the invitation. Finnis was told to go down to the Mermaid's Armpit and talk to Jack. So off Finnis went, down to the docks to find this Jack.

Upon arriving at the Mermaid's Armpit, Finnis took note how seedy the place was. Finnis enter the bar and began scanning the crowd for this "Jack" fello. As his look around he got some glances from some patrons but then he noticed one individual was staring him down. He took this to be Jack. He headed over to where Jack was when he noticed a sharp sting in his side. As Finnis looked down he saw a man standing next to him with a dagger to Finnis side as the man said "always stay alert and know your surroundings". The man withdraw the blade and beckoned Finnis to join him in a private room. Once inside, the man introduced himself as Jack the Blade. Finnis asked about the other guy he thought was Jack. Jack laughs, "he was a teller; I had him stare at you as a distraction". Jack seeing the bewildered look on Finnis face began explaining the ins and outs of the guild structure; how a "teller" was not a member but was an informant under the "protection" of the guild, the eyes and ears of the guild. Jack explained that the guild was officially call The Artisan's guild to give legitimacy and that the ranks within are appropriately named Apprentice, Journeyman, and Master. He also explain the percentages owed to the guild of each rank. Finnis asked about non guild thieves, if they were "punished" for not having permission. Jack said that an independent contractor "could" work without being a member but has to pay for a temporary permit and pay 50% fee on everything pilfered. Jack went further and explain about the assassin's guild. Jack explained that there was an uneasy truce between the two guilds and if you see one of the Nightshadows, turn around and go about your business elsewhere. With that Jack got up telling Finnis "you are free to go but you will report back to me here once a week for the guilds cut of your pilfering, understood?" Finnis nodded in understanding and left.

Finnis meet up with his group and informed them of what transpired. They all were upset and refused to pay the guild anything; it was theirs, it was their way of surviving, and nobody was taking that from them. A week passed and Finnis didn’t show up to meet Jack. That night when the group meet back up, "Tiny" the half orc didn’t show up. They went out to looking for him only to find Jack waiting for them. "What do you want?" one of the half elves asked. "You see, I'm a little disappointed. I explained everything to you Finnis very clearly and you (glancing at Finnis) indicated that you understood but you missed our meeting. Now a lesson must be learned. "Tiny" will not be coming back!" At this, everyone in the group rush forward at Jack but was meet with Jack's lightning reflexes; stabbing several and knocking a few aside. They understood they were outmatched. "Now listen to me, you WILL comply or another will disappear! Then another, and another, until you comply or no one is left!" Jack said sternly as Jack backed into the shadows and disappeared. The group was left there in disbelief. What were they to do? Tiny was dead, and if they didn’t give half of their thieving another of their group would die; but they need all of it to survive. Finnis offered to join the guild as a full member and would pay for his friends so they wouldn’t have to pay. They knew that if Finnis did this then he couldn't be with them. He would belong to the guild and had to do what they wanted. Reluctantly they all agreed and said their farewells.

Over the course of a year, under Jack's tutelage, Finnis had become quite the thief. He was the highest pilfering apprentice in the guild. Many of the Journeyman took notice and Finnis had earn the moniker "The Hand" among them. This caused animosity between other apprentices and Finnis. Within the next six months Finnis attain the rank of Journeyman. Now Finnis could do jobs without Jack babysitting him. He did find one teller able to give him what he wanted. The teller informed him that the city guard raided their hideout, arresting any they captured. Some escaped and went into hiding, but as to where they are now she doesn’t know. Over a few weeks, Finnis was able to track down the tiefling Jandace. She was at the temple of Ilmater. When Finnis got to the temple he found that Father Yorgenson was still there. He was actually tending to Jandace. Jandace had become very sick. She had been living in the sewers and eating rotten food to survive. "Jandace is dying! She has an infection that I cannot heal. She doesn’t have much time left in this world. Go to her, comfort her in her last hours." Father Yorgenson says. Finnis ran to her room and went to her side. Finnis started cry and apologizing for leaving her and the others. As she opened her eyes she placed a hand on Finnis shoulder, she says "it is not your fault, you did the best you could do for us. We would have died long ago if it wasn’t for your aid. We were unfortunate that the guards found us and it had nothing to do with you. I am at peace. Father Yorgenson has been telling me of the tenants of Ilmater." Finnis asks about the others as Jandace's face took on a grim appearance. "The ones that were capture were either executed or sent to labor camps. Father Yorgenson talk with Finnis; helping him cope with his losses. The Father explain that what Finnis did for them was what Ilmater teaches, suffering for others to easy the pains. That was exactly what Finnis did by joining the guild and sending them aid by paying their fees and sending them money. Finnis talk with the Father for a while about Ilmater. Finnis paid for Jandace's funeral.

Getting back to normalcy, Finnis started taking more perilous jobs. On one such job, robbing a moving carriage, he made a flub and spooked the horses cause the carriage to careening out of control through the city. Just as luck would have it, a disheveled person decide to walk across the street at the exact time the carriage came flying by. Finnis, seeing this, leaped up between the horses and launch out in front of them to tackle the dimwit crossing. As Finnis tackled the man, Finnis instinctively rolls along under the carriage trying to avoid any injuries. After the carriage continues on, Finnis manages to stand the guy up asking him "are you daft? That carriage would have killed you if I hadn't been there." He simply replied "that’s what I was hoping it would do." Finnis step back and took a good look at him. He had nothing on but rags with dried blood staining the back of his shirt. Quickly Finnis hurried the man off the street and into the alley way as to not draw attention to themselves. Finnis, using the back alleys, brought the man to Father Yorgenson to care for him. Before leaving Finnis asked the man his name. He replied " Armond ....my name is Armond , Armond Creed." With that Finnis left. Back at the guild hall Finnis had to answer for the botched heist. The Grand Master decide to take all of Finnis's take on the next few heist to repay what guild might of made from the carriage job. Finnis not happy with this decision but could only except it. That night Finnis brood over the thought that it was Armond 's fault that he has to repay the guild. If he had to repay the guild then Armond would have to repay Finnis. So the next morning, Finnis went to the temple of Ilmater seeking Armond . Once he found Armond , Finnis demeanor changed. As he look upon Armond , he could see that he had fresh burns on his left arm no more than a couple weeks old and had several fresh deep lacerations across his back. Father Yorgenson was applying an alchemical medicine to add in healing. Finnis decided to help Armond , he would make him his personal "teller". Once Armond was well enough Finnis pulled some strings and got Armond a job as a dockhand. Armond work the docks help unload cargo keeping an eye and ear on what was coming and going. The job last a month or so just long enough to score a heist or two of rare items be smuggled into the city. That was until Armond accidently got tangled up in some dock lines and fell in the harbor nearly drowning. So Finnis got Armond a job at the Mermaid's Armpit bussing tables. Armond again provided excellent intelligence on who and what was going down in the city. Again Armond had an accident, this time a bar fight broke out and he tried to stop it single handedly being stab several times. Luckily for him they were mere flesh wounds. Finnis, at wits end, ask Father Yorgenson if Armond could do odd jobs around the temple.

{I apologies in advance, I got carried away with the story and couldn’t stop writing. Not all DTs will be this long.}

 

Armond , Barikhol , and Wyer would wake up around the same time every morning around sunrise. Armond would practice his polearm drills. He would remember the things his teachers would impart in him. Chin up, elbows in, balance low, and many other reminders Armond had, but still needed to practice more. So he did, every morning, alongside his friends.  Barikhol would practice his kata making self-corrections on procedure here and there. He would also practice balance exercises, which looked a little funny for a Dwarf, but Barikhol did them well. Wyer would pray. He had his morning meditation rituals and he would ask Tempus for his blessings for the day.  At the end of their morning rituals they would all spar and work up an appetite.

 

Sitting around the breakfast table they talked about what they plan on doing. Armond mentioned that he wants to find out more about the cult activities and there has to be something he can research on them. They couldn’t have appeared out of nowhere. Wyna the waitress tells Armond that he may have luck at Endrith ’s shop, Vallivoe’s Sundries . He collects a lot of things and may have something or know where to get it.

 

Once Armond finishes his meal he informs everyone he is off to do some research. Rowena tells Armond , “If you find a place to get books, see if you can find something on minor gods. I remembered something about the carvings on the knife.” Armond is also interested in finding out where his knife comes from. Armond visited his Patron again about the knife.

 

His Patron said “Oh, I have seen those carvings before. It has something to do with a dead religion. I honestly never paid close attention to it because they were never a problem and I couldn’t use them.” He told Armond the Knife isn’t magical, but it looks like something “mortals” would use to do rituals.

 

His patron didn’t mention anything about a minor god, but Rowena is a researcher so she may have seen something like this before. “If I saw a list of them, I may remember where those are from.” She says.

 

Armond agrees and heads out to Endrith , the rambling gnome. Endrith is not in his shop, Armond discovered this when he approached a locked door and a sign that read “Out Back”. Armond then heard a loud boom and felt the pulse of the explosion.

 

Armond got behind the workshop as fast as he could finding Endrith thrown back into a large pile of hay. Endrith was alive, but had a few minor injuries. “Maybe I am not giving enough space for the pressure I am creating.” Endrith says as Armond helps him out of the stack. “This is going to take me a week to repair this.”

 

Armond asks the obvious question, but is nervous because he know this isn’t going to be an easy answer: “What is this thing?”

 

Endrith , forgetting his wounds, “I introduce you to the Tremendous Aquatic Density Converter Cistern Mechanism!” Endrith stands there with an excited expression on his face. Armond is slightly confused and a little surprised that he is still in the same hour of asking the question.

 

“Ok? But what does it do?” Armond courageously asked as his follow-up question.

 

Endriths face fell, “Honestly? I have no idea. You see this design came to me in a dream. I wasn’t exactly sure what it does, but I knew if I built it I would find its use or it would find its use. Really, you never know with these things . My cousin, his name was Tindrink Donkoargo Finkvizz Draxledwadle Melrond and he invented the famous Melrond’s Foolproof Dishwasher. He had no idea what he was on to and was completely surprised when he figured out what it was that he created… Tindrink Donkoargo Finkvizz Draxledwadle Melrond wasn’t even an engineer, he was a biologist... He was studying this black pudding that he found while adventuring one day (he went on to tell the exact adventure of how he came across it)... Come to find out the pudding was actually a lifeform. It actually eats organic leftovers… After discovering what it ate he started breeding these little things to create the Melrond’s Foolproof Dishwasher (at this point he is now describing where his cousin is famous and where his dishwasher is used)… So sometimes you just need to experiment first and find your invention later.”

 

During that entire exchange Armond tried many times, unsuccessfully, to stop Endrith . Armond finally gave up and had a seat to wait on Endrith to finish. “I need help researching cults or cult activities.” Armond finally said to Endrith , “Do you have any books, information, or anything that would lead me in the right direction?”

 

Endrith looked up at Armond and said “I have been waiting for you to ask.” Armond obviously a little confused because this shouldn’t be a normal question people are asking. “There was a Bard in town, Bellamir , who asked me to give something to you, but to wait till you asked for it.” Armond notices Endrith look at his bracelet he wears on the left arm. It has a sketch of a moon and something else on it. Endrith covered it up with his sleeve. “We roll with the same people. Bellamir said you would want this.” Endrith goes digging through his workspace and pulls out a journal. “He said you will know when you’re ready for this. It should give you a good start on what you need to know.”

 

Endrith paused while Armond started flipping through the pages. It seemed to be a journal on someone studying the cult’s activity. The ink in the journal isn’t that old maybe 5 – 10 years. It is not a lot of information, but it is definitely more than Armond knows. “Without a past, no being can appreciate what they have, and where they may be going.” Armond looks up not familiar with the expression. Endrith answers his unasked question. “It’s something Bellamir would say.”

 

Armond asks “I may as well ask. Do you know where I can go to get some items I think may be magical identified? Also do you have a list, book, or something that names all the minor gods?”

 

“No I don’t have anything about the minor gods… or at least I don’t think I do.” He replies looking around at all his ‘junk’. “You may have better luck at the temple. But Kaylessa , of Swinging Sword , can actually identify magical items. I use her all the time when stuff comes into my shop.”

 

Armond kind of remembers his last encounter with Kaylessa . She seemed a little upset after talking with Armond . She should have communicated better what he needed to do. How was he to know that she would “trust” him? Actually, now that Armond is thinking about it, this is kind of her fault that she got hurt. The diplomatic part of Armond mind was telling him that he isn’t going to get anywhere with her if he goes in there with that argument. It was a misunderstanding.

 

On Endrith 's advice, Armond headed down to Swinging Sword to talk to Kaylessa about identifying the few magic items. The whole way there Armond just kept playing the scenarios over and over again in his head, knowing that his last encounter was not the best, trying to find the best outcome. Stopping outside the door, Armond took one final breath and open the door stepping through. As Armond enter Swinging Sword he could see Kaylessa look up from the bar. As he approached, Kaylessa started around the end of the bar coming to meet him halfway. Just as Kaylessa was about to talk Armond put up his hand "is there somewhere we could talk privately?" Kaylessa a little taken back, motioned to Armond to follow. Kaylessa went to her office in the back and closed the door behind Armond . As she turned around and about to speak again, Armond put his hand up to silence her. Now visibly irritated, Armond spoke quickly. "I am sorry about earlier.
