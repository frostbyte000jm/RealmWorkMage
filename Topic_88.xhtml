Game 12&13

Game Day

Game Date: 1/14/2017

In Play Date:: (10) Marpenoth 18, 1491 ;

TLDR

• Ty recruits the Mud-Bloods to rescue the heroes.

• The Heroes, locked in a earth prison, are undergoing torture to try and be convinced to join the Earth Cult

• The Mud-Bloods have to fight some scary Orogs and an Orc

• They free the Heroes and some slaves

• They are now running form the Monastery being chased by others in the order.

Detailed Entry

At the Mud-blood camp, people in the crowd start noticing the two visitors talking to Joclyn . A couple more approached. These two seemed to recognize one of the visitors, Ty.

 

Ty explained the problem about how his friends were captured and he needs their help. Joclyn and the Mud-Bloods were eager to help, but they themselves were in a bit of a survival problem.

 

Joclyn said she will go and two others who knew Ty volunteered, but Joclyn ordered others to say with the camp, they take priority. They set off the next morning.

 

Back at the Earth Prison:

The three were locked up in a cell. There were two other cells with prisoners in them. Armond thought he may have enough strength to escape. He tried porting out, but stepping outside the prison he landed in what felt like quicksand.

 

The sand drug him back through the rocks and back into the cell. It was extremely painful and he almost suffocated.

 

"Heh Heh Heh Heh." a large creature said. "This place was designed by Earth Mages. Good luck getting past their traps."

 

Hours turned into days, Days turned into Weeks, Weeks turned in to forever. The monsters didn't give time for the prisoners to recover from wounds or rest. They took shifts mining paths. They would be taken near a lava pit and would have to lay there till they would pass out from the pain. They would be beaten, whipped, made to suffocate in quicksand, and more.

 

Hellenrae would visit the heroes. She would try and convince them to join the Earth order. "Look, I don't want to kill you down here in the mines. This is no place for you. Serve me and I can make your lives so much better. Rowena , you already have Earth Magic in you. I can help you control it better. Armond , Wouldn't you rather have your powers not from a source that will defeat you in the end? Barikhol , your monk training is nearly as matched as mine. I can teach you way more than your Genasi Master could. Take the Earths Promise and I will free you from this cell."

 

Armond spat on her. Hellenrae was pissed. The heroes could see her trying to compose herself. "Fine. Let me know when you're ready."

 

The pain didn't let up. Rowena got to the point where she couldn't take it anymore. It has been said that sometimes when people are in captivity, their brains shut down. They go somewhere else. Rowena was visited by Odert . The bard dwarf living in her consciousness. "Lass. Let me take over for a little bit. I can handle this." Rowena trying to protect him acted strong, but Odert reminder her, "We share the same mind. I can see right through you. I want to help. When I can't do it anymore, you take over."

 

Rowena finally agreed and was whisked away to Castle Keep. Here she was at home, but something was different. The library wasn't clean, it didn't have the right smell. It sounded weird even. Then she saw something else in the library. No one else has ever been in her head world before.

 

She chased down the creature and found it was a fox. It was lost and scared. Rowena noticed the fox's ears. They matched her ears perfectly. She reached up to touch her ears and in the real world Odert did the same thing. Except when she and Odert touched her ears, they were different somehow.

 

Rowena knew the fox was dying and she also knew there was nothing she could do. So she just held it and kept telling it. "It's ok. It's ok. I got you."

 

Odert sniffed and scratched his eyes before trying to get a few minutes of sleep.

{If any of you wondered why I didn't find time to type last games notes. I didn't want to relive this moment.}

 

One day a slave was thrown into the cell. He looked at the heroes and started to laugh. The party didn't recognize him at first. He spoke in a deep voice. "Of course you're here. You campions of Red Larch can't keep your noses out of other people's business. I see your dwarf friend is still alive."

 

At this point the heroes have all recognized him. Larrakh , the earth mage from Red Larch . He looks like he has seen better days. "Where is your human and your elf?" he said smugly up to Armond .

 

Armond unloaded what little strength he had and started punching Larrakh over and over in the face. Barakhol and Odert gave each other a look before finally pulling Armond up before he killed Larrakh . Armond would have pulled away before Larrakh died, but Larrakh doesn't need to know that.

 

The mud-bloods and Ty's friend Grimble traveled back to the Stone Monastery. After scouting the place they picked the lock on the back door and traveled into the garden.

 

The garden was full of statues. Some of them angelic like, but others of all different races. The statues were too real, and a couple heroes think they may have once been alive.

 

Trying to escape this room is when the heroes got attacked by two gargoyles. After dispensing of them, they snuck into a room where they found a man working by candle light.

 

After surprising him by a knife to the back, the man pleaded for his life. "Look! I am here against my will. They use me to study magic. I don't want to do that for these monsters! Please don't kill me. Just let me be and I will pretend I never saw you."

 

The heroes chose not to buy that and asked him where they keep the prisoners. The man drew them a map. They also took him with them.

 

Down in the stairs they entered a room where Grimble could sense the undead. The Mud-bloods got ready and tore into the room. The zombies were no match for this crew.

 

"What is on the other side of this door?" one of the half-elves asked.

 

"It is their pet Umber Hulk. He guards the things they collect." the scared man replies.

 

Ty took one of his pitons and make sure that door wasn't going to open. 

 

Otho took a look at the door leading into the cave and informed the rest of the group. This door is stuck. It is going to be loud when we open it.

 

Ty casted a silence spell. "ok, try now." he mouthed. Everyone was already looking around since all the sound went out of the cave. They looked at Ty who was grinning with his new trick.

 

"Cool." Joclyn mouthed and nodded at Otho and Ander to bust open the door. Ty signed in thieves cant to let him and Otho scout ahead.

 

Going out into the hall they ran across these snake like creatures with tentacles and they also ran across these fungi things with tentacles. They also found a Orog guarding some slaves.

 

Ty went back to let the others know. The group objected "We are losing our luck down here. Eventually someone will come by." They decided to sneak down so they can come around the back.

 

Grimble came out and tightened up his equipment so he wouldn't make too much noise sneaking through these caves. When he went to pick up his shield, he dropped it and it did that metal rattling over and over as it spins to a stop on the ground. "You think they heard that?"

 

The door across from them came flying open and an Orog screamed something in Ogre as an arrow flew strait at him. It hit him and they started a fight.

 

In this fight there was 3 orogs and 1 ogre. Grimble charged to blocked the door and the Orog kicked him back to get into the hall. The Ogre squeezed through the door and was swinging his massive club. The other two Orogs ran around back and Otho had to hide. He snuck around the other side barely missing the other Orog who was guarding the slaves.

 

They were running around the outside to flank the heroes from the side. Also, they couldn't fit through the door. This is a design flaw of the dungeon and should be reconsidered while they have slaves to do the work.

 

The heroes continued to fight the Ogre and the Orog. Otho snuck around and attacked the Orog that was following up the rear. He hit him twice and leaped back and ducked out of sight. Otho threw a rock and the Orog followed the sound. Otho jumped out again and stabbed him twice, but didn't get away this time. The Orog knocked him off balance with his great axe. Otho took a quick Jab at the Orog and disappeared into the cave again. "How does this little man do this?" he questioned in Ogre.

 

The rest of the heroes took up against the flank, finishing off the Ogre and the Orog just in time. Grimble and Joclyn were holding off the Orogs when they were hit by a couple unlucky hits. Ty was there to pull them back and get them up to health.

 

Otho appeared behind the Orog that he kept pestering and placed his dagger right through his spine. With a pop, the Orog went down.

 

Ander snuck into the Ogre's room to find the key after finding it he ran down to the slaves pin. He freed them and said "We need to go."

 

The pins next to the one where he saved the others had other slaves in them. Ander looked at them and thought, "There isn't time." He threw them the keys and started to go.

 

Barakhol said "No, we can't just leave them."

 

Rowena was already running over to retrieve the keys and open the cells.

 

Ander rolling his eyes, "Hurry up."

 

The slaves were weak and tired, but they ran as best they could.

 

Ty ran and got the other slaves that were mining in the cave and they ran too.

 

Once they got outside, they made a break for the tree line. From behind them they heard "They went this way! Get them!"
