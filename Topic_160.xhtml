Downtimes FB 06

 

Josephus was hammering the sign "The Tip Toe Hearts" down into the ground. "Another stage another gold piece" Josephus thought to himself. Josephus got pretty comfortable traveling with this performance group. They were kind to him, they paid well, and Josephus got to travel.

 

They were in the town of Loudwater. Josephus was looking around to see what he could do in the town. It was a nice little town, peaceful, clean, and the townsfolk seemed to always know everyone business. It wasn't by any means a small town, but is was smaller than most towns the group performs in. It was fun to listen to some people gossip when it wasn't cruel. Apparently, there was a girl in town who had ears of a fox. Not really sure what they meant Josephus just laughed it off and continued to the middle of town. When he got there Josephus saw there was an event going on, a fish cook-off.

 

Josephus wanted to get in on this action. After looking over the rules and instructions, Josephus went back to the group, borrowed a fishing pole (but all they had was a spear), and went off to get his catch.

 

Josephus had a nice catch and he went back to get to work on his meal. He caught a Lionfish and decided to do a Roasted Spear-Caught Lionfish. He went to the local market and picked up some fresh Island Clams to go with it. The rules said you just had to catch the fish, use what you got. Josephus put a lot of effort into this meal.

 

While making it, Josephus found himself in a daze. He caught himself remembering a time spent with Leaf and Talin down by a river fishing and hanging out together with their feet dangling in the water. It was good times. It is funny how things happen. You think of someone and they appear. Josephus would commonly wish this was true with Leaf. He wasn't even sure if Leaf was still alive. Josephus spun around to grab some supplies and saw a note in his bag.

 

It was small writing, but Josephus could read it. It was in Orcish, but the handwriting was slightly elvish:

I'm alive

It wasn't me

I wish I could have been fishing with you too

Destroy this note

 

There is only one person this note could be from, but why did he leave Josephus a note? Why didn't he come talk to Josephus ?

 

There were three chefs, the one in the middle was a large man, who didn't say a word the entire time. He would take a bite, make a note, and nod. Josephus saw his name card "Fishal".

 

The voting happened and Josephus won. When Josephus was called up to the stage he was shaking the mayors hand and getting congratulations from everyone when Josephus felt a sharp pain in his stomach. "Did I eat something bad?" The crowd must have noticed too, because there were gasp of shock. Josephus looked down and saw he had a crossbow bolt hanging out of him. "How did that get there?"

 

Looking out into the crowd Josephus saw a man in a cloak pull his hood a little tighter and disappear into the crowd.

 

Two days later Josephus woke up in the temple. A nurse saw him stir, "Careful now, you don't want to aggravate your wounds." she helped him sit up. "You're going to want to take it easy for a couple days, the healer said nothing serious got punctured so you should recover quickly. You're quite luck… sorry. I guess from your point of view getting hit with a bolt isn't lucky."

 

After Josephus recovered he talked with The Tip Toe Hearts and collected his winnings. He was going to take some time off to hunt down who tried to kill him.

Downtime 05

 

Josephus was helping a group called the Tip Toe Hearts set up stage. They paid him well traveling with them, and Josephus had further to go. He didn't mind waiting around in a new town for a couple days for the group to put on their show. The group was trust worthy and good company. Josephus needed good company. During his quiet moments Josephus can still see his men choking and dying from the nightshade. So Josephus tries not to have too many quiet times.

 

The work was more difficult today because it was raining. This shit is hard enough to setup in perfect outdoors conditions, but add in being wet, stepping in mud, and shivering from the cold and you are going to have a difficult day.

 

It was worth it. That night was clear skies as the gods shined the stars and the moon down on them. Their performance was spectacular, as always. After the show the streets were full of patrons heading back to their homes or off to a tavern for a few more.

 

The next morning Josephus woke to see some members of the troop a little distraught. After asking them what is worrying them, they told him about the attack last night.

 

Josephus made his way to town and to the room where it happened. The local sheriff looked at him and one of the troop leaders told the sheriff, "You can trust him. Josephus helps protect us." The sheriff and the troop leader must know each other, because the sheriff didn't hesitate in letting Josephus in on what happened.

 

The sheriff briefs Josephus , "I have heard rumors of this kind of attack. The downstairs neighbor told me that she heard someone playing what sounds like the pipes. There wasn't any other noise, but somehow the attacker tied a woman to the bed, and cut her open. If this doesn't give you the creeps, let me… are you ok around dead bodies?" The sheriff asks. After a nod of assurance from Josephus he continues, "The victims face is not as expected.”

 

He shows Josephus the woman. She was tied to the bed, naked, bloodied from being cut open, but her face was surprising. She had the look of pure pleasure. Like everything was perfect. This is more nerve racking then any death Josephus has ever seen.

 

In the room was a doctor inspecting the body. He tells the sheriff, "She is missing some bones. They were removed. The way her body looks, this was done with finesses."

 

The sheriff tells Josephus , "The killer is being call Windharrow. The only thing people have put together of him is that someone hears music coming from a pipe flute the night before the body is found. Take care of this troupe. They are my friends."

 

After Josephus left he reached into his pocket and pulled out the note he accidently lifted from one of his friends. "By orders of General Zanther, I hereby command a soldier from {Still need Army Name} to execute Harald Grayspear aka Windharrow. Windharrow is a known cult member of the Cult of Howling Hatred and has been seen in the presence of Aerisi Kalinoth. This mission needs to be done under stealth."

 

They traveled on and the troupe once again paid Josephus for traveling with them.

Downtime 04

 

Josephus wakes up the next morning and decides he is going to need some additional coin. He thinks it is about time he perfect Crumblecake.

 

Crumblecakes are moist loaves from nuts, chickpea mash, chopped roots and greens, turkey and wildfowl scraps, all baked together.

 

They are bland at best, but they are nourishing. The original maker was actually on to something. One loaf could work as your rations for a very long trip. It gives you what you need to survive, but it doesn’t fulfill that pleasure sense of good food.

 

Josephus stops into Mangobarl Lorren Bakery. His daughter is working behind the counter today with her father. Juli-anne meet the characters on their first trip into Red Larch . She greets Josephus when he comes in.

 

"if it isn't Mr Crumblecake!" she says with a smile. "Come to take another crack?" Her father chuckles a little.

 

"Really it is a bland food, I don't think it can be made better. Why don't we cook something else?" he says knowing Josephus is the stubborn type.

 

Josephus tells Magobarl, "We are on to something. Nothing will last as long as a loaf of crumblecake, you have many visitors in the town and they are all on long travels. It is called the long road for a reason. If we can make Crumblecake at least palatable, people will want it, because it will last."

 

Juli-anne tells Josephus , "ok… what can we do?"

 

They go to work and start messing with many different things they have locally in the north. Juli-anne and Josephus go to the market together. While they are finishing up they meet Grund .

 

Grund is a half or pickle vendor.  “Orc Fingers! Get your Orc Fingers! I got em fresh for ye!” Josephus noticed kids would try and sneak off with some from the cart. Most of the time they were caught by Grund , but a couple times they would succeed. Grund would see them once it is too late and he would shake his fist at them and say “I gonna tell ye mum.” Then he would chuckle. Josephus noticed it was a game. If the children were caught they would pay up, but if they succeeded they won a pickle, err Orc Finger, for free.

 

"Oh Grund you're never going to make a profit with this kids liberating your supplies." Juli-anne says to him.

 

"It OK. I know der mum and dads. They throw me a coin or two to make up for their boys a playing. They don't mean no harm." Grund says. "Would you like an orc finger?"

 

"You know it is kind of morbid you call them that, being…" Juli-anne started.

 

"What?" Grund said "Being what?"

 

"well…" she replied "being half orc."

 

"I am not. You take that back." Grund says with a smile. "You know I am more like two halfs orc. Which makes me closer to the human side."

 

Josephus not being a math expert still feels this isn't right. "Whatever you say." Juli-anne says as she nudges Josephus to start moving along.

 

As they start walking Josephus feels Juli-anne reach for his hand. He looks down and sees that she is placing a pickle in his palm. She looks at Josephus scandalously and takes a bite and turns around to tell Grund buy. "Have a good day Grund ." she says with a mouthful of pickle.

 

"What the? How did… Juli-anne I know where you work!" he says laughing.

 

She pats her side "Check your pockets!" Grund does and he pulls out a few coins. "You stay sharp and maybe you will catch me next time."

 

They get back to the shop and start baking. This went on for a few days when finally they hit it. They make a batch that actually has some taste. It is by no means great, but it isn't terrible. The selling point is it will last longer than any loaf of bread.

 

Mangobarl Lorren taste it and tells Josephus that he thinks he can sell this.

 

A couple days later Josephus come back into the shop to see how it did. Mangobarl Lorren pulls out some coins and says, "Here is your share. Thank you for the help."

Downtime FB 02

 

During some point while Armond is adventuring off to find Barikhol , Josephus decides to adventure off to try and earn some coin. Josephus is a grand cook, from the first time he lad his hands on a wooden spoon he knew that it was only natural. At some point in his life he realized that being in a kitchen wasn’t enough and this lead him on to become an adventurer.

 

Josephus told Wyer that he was going to head to Waterdeep for a couple weeks and he is welcome to join him if he wants to. Wyer , having nothing better to do and wanting to find a temple to pray at, decided to join him. The journey took a couple of days and along the way they stayed at a couple inns. Josephus and Wyer noticed that the roads were much more dangerous these days than they have been.

 

Once Josephus arrived in WaterDeep he set off looking for employment. It took a few restaurants that turned him down before he found ‘The Codfather’. There was a sign that read, “need cook with skills”. A man on the inside explained that their normal, world renowned, cook has had an unexpected death in the family and had to travel. The owner, Mr Fishal, will meet Josephus in the kitchen.

 

Josephus doesn’t normally get to work with seafood and took this as a unique challenge. Before getting started he walked the kitchen familiarizing himself with everything. The owner watched him quizzically while Josephus would do things like tap on a pan or test the flames from the grill.  Josephus pulled out many different samples of foods and would take a small taste of a few he was unfamiliar with. After about 10 minutes of this Josephus said “I am ready.”

 

He got started gathering, mixing, preheating, seasoning, and arranging. By this time a few other kitchen staff stood around to watch him work. A waiter saw what was happening and went to get a table and chair for the owner. A few staff members started taking notes and asking questions, trying to keep them brief to not disturb this new cook. Finally, Josephus finished his dish and started to bring it to the table, when a staff member stepped in front of him stopping his progress.

 

It was a young girl around the age of 19. She takes the tongs out of Josephus hands, moves somethings around on the plate, grabs a flower, and places it on the plate. Josephus had a tough time looking away from this girl. She had the most beautiful face that he has seen in a very long time. When he finally looked in the almost awkward moment of silence Josephus could see that by adding this garnish and rearranging the food she drastically improved the appearance of his dish. Seeing the look on Josephus face of understanding the girl smiled and kindly moved out of the way.

 

Josephus placed the plate in front of the owner and he stared at it for a very long time. He sniffed it, spun it, stood up and walked around the table to see it from other angles. Josephus has never had his food appraised so precisely in his life, and never this much before someone actually taking a bite. The worst part is the owner never said a word. Not one word from the moment they stepped into the kitchen.

 

Josephus about to say something to break the silence was nudged by the cute girl. Taking a look at her and the girl not looking back at him he could see the most imperceptible shake of the head. The owner took his first bite, stood up, and walked out of the kitchen. Josephus truly thinking he has screwed up. He said nothing. Not one thing. This was the rudest he has experience that he can remember. He could have at least told him what it is that he wanted. At this moment everyone started to clap.

 

Josephus obviously confused looks around trying to make sense of all this. “He didn’t tell you to get out. That means you got the job. My name is Joane, we should get started preparing what is for dinner.” Joane is exceptionally beautiful, with brown hair and dark amber eyes. She is wearing her kitchen uniform, but has a neckless with a cog shaped locket that dangles on her chess. Joane speaks with a timid voice, but walks with confidence.

 

One night during dinner Josephus was cleaning up and saw a tab for a familiar name. A man by the name of “Harald Grayspear” apparently had eaten here (See Downtime 01). After Josephus shift he tried to see if he could find out more information on Grayspear, but he apparently already left town. The rest of the week, Josephus couldn’t prove it, but he felt like someone was watching him.

 

The rest of the week working in the kitchen was amazing, but all good things come to an end. The previous cook returned and was very gracious for Josephus taking over the kitchen. They told Josephus anytime he is back in Waterdeep to come by. Joane gave Josephus her contact information, and said to look her up when he is back in town.
