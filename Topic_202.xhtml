Gar

Overview

Real Name: Gar Shatterkeel

Gender: Male

Race: Human

Class: None

Age: Adult

Character Role: Neutral

RolePlay Notes: Gar is a sullen man who says little, letting his actions speak for him. He believes he has been wronged by the world and hates all people except those as broken and unfortunate as himself. Gar also despises weakness in others.

Gar is covered in barnacles, and he wears an artificial arm in the shape of a crab’s claw over the stump of his left arm.

Profile

Face of: Cult of the Crushing Wave

Story

Gar was born in a poor fishing village in the Nelanther Isles. At a young age, he lost his family to a Sahuagin attack. The sea devils slaughtered almost everyone he knew. A Tethyrian merchant galley conscripted the young orphan into service, and Gar was forced into a brutal indentured servitude that was barely better than being enslaved. His unwilling service came to an end when pirates attacked the merchant ship. During the fighting, Gar was knocked overboard. For days he survived by clinging to the ship’s wreckage, until a shark ripped off his left arm. Death seemed imminent, but a powerful current arose and bore him away from the hungry predator. Within an hour he was cast upon the shores of the Sword Coast.

 

Gar believed that the current that had saved his life was an elemental sent by the sea. From that day forward, he devoted himself to the ocean, teaching himself its secrets and learning powerful water magic over years of lonely wandering. He despised all other people, remembering only his harsh treatment by the merchant crew and the brutality of the pirates. But in time he became aware of a call to a higher purpose, impelling him to find other examples of human flotsam and teach them about the power of the sea.

 

Gar’s visions led him to the secret waters beneath the Sumber Hills. He found a hidden cavern temple, on whose altar lay a powerful magical trident. Sensing its power, Gar took up the weapon and began to call his cult to join him in the Temple of the Crushing Wave.

Olhydra , the princess of Evil Water: When she was blessed with the power of the Elemental Evil she came to Faerun to see for herself what followers she would prefer. The first people she saw was a fishermen’s boat.

 

On board there was a boy. He was the son of an abusive father. His father would cut him, punch him, and he would instruct his crew to help ‘make a man’ out of his son and do the same. He would throw his son overboard during storms to make him strong and swim the sea. He would lock his son in a box when he would find him playing. He took his child’s shoes on hot days and the boy would have to burn his feet trying to make his way across the boat. The boy hated life and hated everyone in the world, for life on the boat was all he knew.

 

One day the boat was attacked by pirates. Olhydra looked down and saw the boy holding a knife pointedly at one of the pirates. The pirate threw him overboard and Olhydra caught him. ‘You will be my prince.’ She said to him. ‘I shall name you Gar.’ When she splashed Gar back onto the fisherman’s boat his father and the crew had been killed.

 

The pirates turned to this boy and the one that threw him over before came walking up to him laughing ‘Boy! You are a stubborn fellow. You would do fine on my crew.’ The child shook his head and attacked. With the full power of Olhydra behind him the captain never stood a chance and dropped quicker than anyone would believe. He turned to the rest of the men and said in a wavy voice ‘You follow us now!’

 

Over the years Gar devoted himself to the ocean, learning its secrets and powerful water magic. He loved his crew and taught many of them water tricks. He despised any that were not his crew, remembering only his harsh treatment by the merchant crew and the brutality of the pirates. But in time he became aware of a call to a higher purpose, impelling him to find other examples of human flotsam and teach them about the power of the sea.

 

Gar’s visions led him to the secret waters beneath the Sumber Hills. He found a hidden cavern temple, on whose altar lay a powerful magical trident. Sensing its power, Gar took up the weapon and began to call his cult to join him in the Temple of the Crushing Wave.​

 

Additional Details

He wields the elemental weapon Drown, a trident imbued with the essence of Olhydra, Princess of Evil Water.
